## 🛑 EXHAUSTIVE DETAILS OF THE 8 SECTIONS (40 QUESTIONS BANK)

`passage.md` (Silverline Editorial) से निम्नलिखित 8 अनुभागों में ठीक 5-5 प्रश्न तैयार किए जाएंगे:

### SECTION 1: Error Detection (Q1 to Q5)
*   **Pedagogical Objective:** Test absolute command over high-level grammar rules (Subjunctive Mood, Inversion, Correlative Conjunctions, and Subject-Verb Agreement with intervening phrases).
*   **Structure:** A single sentence divided into five parts: `(A) / (B) / (C) / (D) / (E) No error`.
*   **Triggers & Rules:**
    *   *Subjunctive Mood:* e.g., "KSSP recommended that the government modernizes (X) -> **modernize** (V1) the existing signal system."
    *   *Inversion:* e.g., "Not only did the government derail (V1) the project, but they also explored (V2)..."
    *   *Intervening Clauses:* Subject and verb separated by parenthetical phrases (e.g., "along with", "as well as").
*   **Explanation Standard:** Provide a Devanagari translation of the correct sentence, a detailed deconstruction table for all 5 parts, a clean grammar rule card, and an exam hack to identify the error under 45 seconds.

### SECTION 2: Sentence Improvement (Q6 to Q10)
*   **Pedagogical Objective:** Test syntactic fluency, parallel structure, and conditional inversions.
*   **Structure:** A sentence containing a **bolded segment**. Five options (A to E) are provided, where (A) is the original and (B, C, D, E) are grammatical improvements.
*   **Triggers & Rules:**
    *   *Conditional Clause Inversions (Third Conditional):* e.g., "**Had the Union government approved** the project, the Left Democratic Front would have..."
    *   *Double Comparatives:* e.g., "**The deeper the ecological concerns, the more controversial** the rail line becomes."
    *   *Parallelism in Verb Forms:* Ensuring Infinitives and Gerunds align perfectly across conjunctions (e.g., "not only to modernize... but also to strengthen...").
*   **Explanation Standard:** Detailed breakdown of why each distractor fails in parallelism or tense consistency.

### SECTION 3: Para Jumbles / Sentence Rearrangement (Q11 to Q15)
*   **Pedagogical Objective:** Assess logical coherence, discourse markers, and pronoun-noun relationships in academic paragraphs.
*   **Structure:** A set of 5-6 sentences labeled (A, B, C, D, E, F) to be rearranged into a coherent paragraph. Five questions follow: Q11 (First), Q12 (Second), Q13 (Third), Q14 (Fourth), Q15 (Last).
*   **Triggers & Rules:**
    *   Sentences must contain strong semantic linkages (e.g., *Instead, Rather, Consequently, This is because, Thus, However*).
    *   Must have a clear, single logical sequence (e.g., C-A-E-B-D) with no ambiguity.
*   **Explanation Standard:** Write a full explanation for the entire paragraph sequence in one place, showing how each sentence links to the next.

### SECTION 4: Fill in the Blanks (Q16 to Q20)
*   **Pedagogical Objective:** Test vocabulary usage, context-clues, and polysemy (multiple meanings of words).
*   **Structure: Triple-Sentence Single-Blank Fillers**
    *   Provide three completely distinct sentences (based on or inspired by the passage), each containing one blank space.
    *   Five options (A to E) of single words are provided. Only one word must fit logically and grammatically in all three sentences.
*   **Triggers & Rules:**
    *   Target words: *Precipitate, Compromise, Recoil, Sanction, Buffer, Decongest, Fillip*.
    *   Example: Sentence I (economic buffer), Sentence II (shock absorber buffer), Sentence III (buffer state).
*   **Explanation Standard:** Show the definition of the correct word and explain its semantic role in all three contexts.

### SECTION 5: Synonyms (Q21 to Q25)
*   **Pedagogical Objective:** Test contextual vocabulary and shifts in word meaning (Noun vs. Verb vs. Adjective).
*   **Structure: Context-Based Vocabulary Shifts (Different Meaning)**
    *   Provide a Principal Sentence (PS) from the editorial containing a **bolded word** (e.g., *track, gross, draft, weather, derail*).
    *   Provide three Sub-Sentences (I, II, III) using the same word in different contexts.
    *   Options: Choose the sub-sentence(s) where the word is used in a **different meaning or part of speech** compared to the PS.
*   **Explanation Standard:** Identify the part of speech and exact definition of the word in the PS and each of the three sub-sentences.

### SECTION 6: Antonyms (Q26 to Q30)
*   **Pedagogical Objective:** Test contextual opposites of academic vocabulary.
*   **Structure:** A sentence from the passage is provided with a bolded word. Students must choose the exact contextual antonym from five options.
*   **Triggers & Rules:**
    *   Target words: *delegitimised, irreversible, underplayed, brazenly, congestion*.
    *   Options must include deceptive near-synonyms as distractors.
*   **Explanation Standard:** Explain the core meaning of the target word in the sentence's context, followed by the logical opposite meaning of the correct antonym.

### SECTION 7: Collocation (Q31 to Q35)
*   **Pedagogical Objective:** Test native word combinations (Adverb-Verb, Verb-Preposition, Noun-Adjective).
*   **Structure:** Blank filling or error detection targeting fixed pairs.
*   **Triggers & Rules:**
    *   *Fixed Prepositions:* e.g., "incapable **of cultivating**" (not "to cultivate").
    *   *Verb-Noun pairs:* e.g., "**bear** the burden" (not "carry"), "**give** a fillip" (not "make").
    *   *Adverb-Verb pairs:* e.g., "**brazenly** pushing ahead".
*   **Explanation Standard:** Highlight the standard collocations and why other combinations sound unnatural.

### SECTION 8: Reading Comprehension (Q36 to Q40)
*   **Pedagogical Objective:** Test high-level inferential reasoning, comprehension of central arguments, and tone analysis.
*   **Structure:** 5 multiple-choice questions (A to E) based directly on the themes of `passage.md`.
*   **Triggers & Rules:**
    *   *Inferential Questions:* "What does the author imply by...?"
    *   *Tone Analysis:* Identify the author's attitude (e.g., *critical, skeptical, cautious*).
    *   *Central Thesis:* Identify the core argument of the editorial.
*   **Explanation Standard:** Deconstruct why incorrect options represent extreme traps, partial truths, or out-of-context facts.
