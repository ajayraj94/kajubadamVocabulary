#### Q41. Narration — Assertive Sentence with Tense Changes
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The executive stated, "China is weaponising technology in this trade war and we have had to stop our local assembly operations."

**Options:**
(A) The executive stated that China was weaponising technology in that trade war and they have had to stop their local assembly operations. 
(B) The executive stated that China was weaponising technology in that trade war and they had had to stop their local assembly operations. 
(C) The executive stated that China is weaponising technology in this trade war and they had had to stop their local assembly operations. 
(D) The executive stated that China was weaponising technology in this trade war and we had had to stop our local assembly operations. 
(E) The executive stated if China was weaponising technology in that trade war and they had had to stop their local assembly operations. 

**Correct - B**
**Reasoning:**
When the reporting verb is in the past tense ('stated'): (1) the present continuous 'is weaponising' backshifts to 'was weaponising'; (2) the demonstrative 'this' shifts to 'that'; (3) the pronoun 'we' changes to 'they' and 'our' changes to 'their'; (4) the present perfect 'have had to' backshifts to 'had had to'. Option B correctly applies all these shifts.

#### Q44. Narration — Modal "Must" Retention
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

### Q4. What is the best strategy to solve Active & Passive Voice in bank exams?

**Answer:** Active and passive voice questions in banking Mains exams are highly structural and test complex verb phrases. Follow this **5-step strategy**:

1. **Locate the Primary Verb Auxiliary Chain:** Break down the active verb construction (e.g., *is implementing* - present continuous) and map it directly to its exact passive equivalent (*is being implemented*).
2. **Handle Perfect Modals with "Been":** Perfect modals like *ought to have warned* require the introduction of *been* in the passive voice (*ought to have been warned*). Ensure the modal verb itself does not change (e.g., do not change *ought to* to *must*).
3. **Remember the Causative Verb 'To' Trap:** Passive structures using causative verbs like *make* must be followed by a full 'to-infinitive' (e.g., *were made to undergo*), even though the active structure uses a bare infinitive.
4. **Isolate Relative and Modifying Clauses:** Do not transform the voice of subordinate relative clauses (e.g., *that enables Beijing...*) unless necessary. Only the main clause's verb should undergo voice change.
5. **Analyze the Agent in Passive-to-Active Reversals:** Identify the logical subject from the *by*-phrase, match its singular/plural status with the correct active verb form, and ensure tense consistency.
