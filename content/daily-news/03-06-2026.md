यह यात्रा विशेष महत्व **धारण करती है** (*विशेष महत्व या गुणवत्ता को अपनाना या ग्रहण करना*) क्योंकि यह 3 जनवरी को अमेरिकी बलों द्वारा वेनेजुएला के राष्ट्रपति निकोलस मादुरो को पकड़े जाने के पांच महीने बाद हो रही है, जिसके बाद पूर्व उपराष्ट्रपति रोड्रिगेज को देश का कार्यवाहक राष्ट्रपति नियुक्त किया गया था।

"The discussions will involve the full **SPECTRUM** (*a wide range of related qualities, ideas, or activities*) of India-Venezuela relations and involve and explore **AVENUES** (*ways, pathways, or directions of approach or progress*) for further cooperation in the areas of energy, trade, investment, pharmaceuticals, healthcare, transportation and renewable energy," the MEA spokesperson said.

30 जनवरी को प्रधानमंत्री नरेंद्र मोदी ने वेनेजुएला की कार्यवाहक राष्ट्रपति डेल्सी रोड्रिगेज से बात की और दोनों नेता आने वाले वर्षों में द्विपक्षीय संबंधों को नई **ऊंचाइयों** (*सफलता, उपलब्धि या स्थिति का उच्च स्तर*) पर ले जाने पर सहमत हुए।

### ENERGY SECURITY AND CRUDE TRADE

India is an important partner of Venezuela in the areas of energy and investment.  
भारत ऊर्जा और निवेश के क्षेत्र में वेनेजुएला का एक महत्वपूर्ण भागीदार है।

Indian public sector undertakings have made significant investment in Venezuela in the energy sector, and they are keen to explore more opportunities to further enhance their presence, the MEA spokesperson said.  
भारतीय सार्वजनिक क्षेत्र के उपक्रमों ने वेनेजुएला के ऊर्जा क्षेत्र में महत्वपूर्ण निवेश किया है और वे अपनी उपस्थिति को और बढ़ाने के लिए अधिक अवसरों की तलाश करने के इच्छुक हैं, विदेश मंत्रालय के प्रवक्ता ने कहा।

India — specifically private sector refining giant Reliance Industries (RIL) — was a regular buyer of Venezuelan crude prior to the **IMPOSITION** (*the action of officially introducing a law, tax, or sanction*) of US sanctions on Caracas in 2019.  
भारत — विशेष रूप से निजी क्षेत्र की दिग्गज रिफाइनिंग कंपनी रिलायंस इंडस्ट्रीज (आरआईएल) — 2019 में काराकास पर अमेरिकी प्रतिबंधों के **लगाए जाने** (*किसी कानून, कर या प्रतिबंध को आधिकारिक तौर पर लागू करने की क्रिया*) से पहले वेनेजुएला के कच्चे तेल का एक नियमित खरीदार था।

Following the sanctions, oil imports from Venezuela stopped within a few months.  
प्रतिबंधों के बाद, वेनेजुएला से तेल का आयात कुछ ही महीनों में बंद हो गया।

As per India's official trade data, Caracas was New Delhi's fifth-largest supplier of oil in 2019, providing close to 16 million tonnes of crude to Indian refiners.  
भारत के आधिकारिक व्यापार आंकड़ों के अनुसार, 2019 में काराकास नई दिल्ली का पांचवां सबसे बड़ा तेल आपूर्तिकर्ता था, जिसने भारतीय रिफाइनर को लगभग 16 मिलियन टन कच्चे तेल की आपूर्ति की थी।

2019-20 में भारत और वेनेजुएला के बीच द्विपक्षीय व्यापार $6.40 बिलियन था, जिसमें से मुख्य रूप से कच्चे तेल का भारतीय आयात $6.06 बिलियन का था।

Since February, after Maduro was captured and Rodriguez became the Acting President under the new **REGIME** (*a system of government or administration, especially an authoritarian one*), India has resumed oil imports from Venezuela, especially after the war in West Asia has **DISRUPTED** (*interrupted or threw into disorder*) the energy supply passing through the Strait of Hormuz.  
फरवरी से, जब मादुरो को पकड़ा गया और रोड्रिगेज नई **शासन** (*सरकार या प्रशासन की प्रणाली*) व्यवस्था के तहत कार्यवाहक राष्ट्रपति बनीं, भारत ने वेनेजुएला से तेल आयात फिर से शुरू कर दिया है, विशेष रूप से तब जब पश्चिम एशिया में युद्ध ने होर्मुज जलडमरूमध्य से गुजरने वाली ऊर्जा आपूर्ति को **बाधित** (*बाधा उत्पन्न करना या अव्यवस्थित करना*) कर दिया है।

Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Rodriguez agreed that energy security remains a critical anchor of their diplomatic engagement.  
प्रधानमंत्री नरेंद्र मोदी और रोड्रिगेज इस बात पर सहमत हुए कि ऊर्जा सुरक्षा उनके राजनयिक जुड़ाव का एक महत्वपूर्ण आधार बनी हुई है।

#### Q6. Spotting the Error — Collective Subject & Intervening Modifiers
*Section: Advanced Spotting the Error — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*
*(Source: Daily Editorial Analysis | Curated by kajubadamvocabulary.in)*

**Question:**
The delegation of Venezuelan ministers, which includes the foreign affairs spokesperson (I) / and several high-ranking financial advisors from Caracas, (II) / have recently arrived in New Delhi to initiate bilateral discussions. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I) 
(B) Only (II) 
(C) Only (III) 
(D) Both (I) and (III) 
(E) None of (I), (II) & (III)



**Correct - C**
**Reasoning:**

#### Q41. Narration — Assertive Dual Tense Backshift
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
Randhir Jaiswal stated, "The Acting President is visiting India for a working visit because the International Big Cats Alliance summit has been deferred."

**Options:**
(A) Randhir Jaiswal stated that the Acting President is visiting India for a working visit because the International Big Cats Alliance summit has been deferred. 
(B) Randhir Jaiswal stated that the Acting President was visiting India for a working visit because the International Big Cats Alliance summit had been deferred. 
(C) Randhir Jaiswal stated that the Acting President was visiting India for a working visit because the International Big Cats Alliance summit has been deferred. 
(D) Randhir Jaiswal stated that the Acting President is visiting India for a working visit because the International Big Cats Alliance summit had been deferred. 
(E) Randhir Jaiswal stated whether the Acting President was visiting India for a working visit because the International Big Cats Alliance summit had been deferred. 

**Correct - B**
**Reasoning:**
Because the reporting verb is in the past tense ('stated'): (1) the present continuous 'is visiting' backshifts to the past continuous 'was visiting'; (2) the present perfect passive 'has been deferred' backshifts to the past perfect passive 'had been deferred'. Option B applies both backshifts correctly.

#### Q42. Narration — Interrogative Sentence
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The journalist asked the spokesperson, "Why did India identify Delcy Rodriguez as the Acting President instead of the President of Venezuela?"

**Options:**
(A) The journalist asked the spokesperson why did India identify Delcy Rodriguez as the Acting President instead of the President of Venezuela. 
(B) The journalist asked the spokesperson why India had identified Delcy Rodriguez as the Acting President instead of the President of Venezuela. 
(C) The journalist asked the spokesperson that why India had identified Delcy Rodriguez as the Acting President instead of the President of Venezuela. 
(D) The journalist asked the spokesperson why had India identified Delcy Rodriguez as the Acting President instead of the President of Venezuela. 
(E) The journalist asked the spokesperson why India identified Delcy Rodriguez as the Acting President instead of the President of Venezuela. 

**Correct - B**
**Reasoning:**
When converting a 'wh-' interrogative sentence to indirect speech: (1) the question word 'why' acts as the conjunction, and adding 'that' is incorrect (eliminating C); (2) the word order shifts from interrogative (verb-subject) to assertive (subject-verb), making A and D incorrect; (3) the simple past 'did identify' backshifts to the past perfect 'had identified' (eliminating E). Option B complies with all these rules.

#### Q43. Narration — Negative Imperative with Demonstrative Shifts
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The spokesperson warned the delegates, "Do not finalize this bilateral agreement without exploring these alternative avenues for crude oil trade."

**Options:**
(A) The spokesperson warned the delegates to not finalize that bilateral agreement without exploring those alternative avenues for crude oil trade. 
(B) The spokesperson warned the delegates not to finalize this bilateral agreement without exploring these alternative avenues for crude oil trade. 
(C) The spokesperson warned the delegates not to finalize that bilateral agreement without exploring those alternative avenues for crude oil trade. 
(D) The spokesperson warned the delegates that they should not finalize that bilateral agreement without exploring those alternative avenues for crude oil trade. 
(E) The spokesperson warned the delegates not to have finalized that bilateral agreement without exploring those alternative avenues for crude oil trade. 

**Correct - C**
**Reasoning:**
For negative imperatives: (1) 'Do not' changes to 'not to' in indirect speech; (2) the demonstrative 'this' shifts to 'that'; (3) 'these' shifts to 'those'. Option C correctly applies all these rules. Option A uses the split infinitive 'to not finalize', and Option B fails to shift the demonstratives.

#### Q44. Narration — Modal "Must" Retention
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The minister observed, "Every developing nation must secure stable energy channels to protect its domestic economic interests from external shocks."

**Options:**
(A) The minister observed that every developing nation must secure stable energy channels to protect its domestic economic interests from external shocks. 
(B) The minister observed that every developing nation had to secure stable energy channels to protect its domestic economic interests from external shocks. 
(C) The minister observed that every developing nation must have secured stable energy channels to protect its domestic economic interests from external shocks. 
(D) The minister observed that every developing nation should secure stable energy channels to protect its domestic economic interests from external shocks. 
(E) The minister observed that every developing nation would secure stable energy channels to protect its domestic economic interests from external shocks. 

**Correct - A**
**Reasoning:**
The modal verb 'must' remains unchanged in indirect speech when it expresses a permanent rule, continuous duty, or universal advice that remains valid at the time of reporting. Since securing stable energy channels is a continuous principle, 'must' is retained. Changing it to 'had to' would imply a past, completed obligation. Thus, Option A is the correct choice.

#### Q45. Narration — Exclamatory Sentence
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
"What an unusual transition of power this capture has triggered in South America!" exclaimed the political analyst.

**Options:**
(A) The political analyst exclaimed with surprise what an unusual transition of power that capture had triggered in South America. 
(B) The political analyst exclaimed that that capture had triggered a very unusual transition of power in South America.

(E) The political analyst exclaimed with surprise that that capture had triggered a very unusual transition of power in South America. 

**Correct - E**
**Reasoning:**
To convert an exclamatory sentence starting with 'What a...' into indirect speech: (1) change the structure into an assertive one using intensifiers like 'very'; (2) change 'this' to 'that'; (3) backshift the present perfect 'has triggered' to the past perfect 'had triggered'; (4) use an appropriate reporting verb with emotion (e.g., *exclaimed with surprise*). Option E correctly implements all these changes. Option B lacks the emotional expression, and Option C fails to backshift.

### Q4. What is the best strategy to solve Active & Passive Voice in bank exams?

**Answer:** Active & Passive Voice transformations are a high-scoring topic in IBPS PO and SBI PO Mains. Follow this **5-step strategy**:

1. **Deconstruct the Auxiliary Verb Chain:** For complex passive structures like *are yet to be formally evaluated*, map them back to the active structure *is/are yet to formally evaluate*. Pay attention to singular or plural subject concord.
2. **Handle Modal Perfect Auxiliary Verbs:** Ensure that active phrases like *ought to have invited* are converted to *ought to have been invited* by introducing the auxiliary *been*. Do not change the modal verb itself.
3. **Master Causative structures:** Remember that verbs like *make* take a bare infinitive in the active voice (*made the refiners resume*), but require a full to-infinitive in the passive voice (*were made to resume*). This is a very common Mains trap.
4. **Preserve Subordinate relative clauses:** If a sentence has a relative clause (*prompting Russia to criticize*), keep its voice active in the options, as transforming it unnecessarily often results in awkward or ungrammatical sentences.
5. **Identify the Logical Agent:** In passive-to-active transformations, identify the logical subject from the *by*-phrase, make it the main subject of your active clause, and verify verb-subject agreement.
