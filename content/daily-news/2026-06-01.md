हालांकि, वह अलग सर्ज फंड उस रूप में **साकार** (वास्तविक रूप में सामने आना या पूरा होना) नहीं हो सका।

#### Q7. Spotting the Error — Subjunctive Mood & Parallelism
*Section: Advanced Spotting the Error — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The managing partners strongly recommended that the venture capital firm (I) / completely reorganizes its seed-investing platform, which would not only streamline (II) / operations but also reducing unnecessary executive overhead. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Both (I) and (II) 
(B) Both (II) and (III) 
(C) Only (II) 
(D) Only (III) 
(E) All (I), (II), & (III)



**Correct - B**

#### Q41. Narration — Assertive Dual Tense Backshift
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
Rajan Anandan stated, "Aaditya Sood is very much with Peak XV but has transitioned to an advisory role."

**Options:**
(A) Rajan Anandan stated that Aaditya Sood is very much with Peak XV but has transitioned to an advisory role.

(E) Rajan Anandan stated whether Aaditya Sood was very much with Peak XV but had transitioned to an advisory role. 

**Correct - B**
**Reasoning:**
Because the reporting verb is in the past tense ('stated'), all present tense verbs in the quotation must undergo backshift. The simple present 'is' changes to 'was', and the present perfect 'has transitioned' changes to 'had transitioned'. Option B correctly applies both backshifts independently.

#### Q42. Narration — Interrogative Wh-Question
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The journalist asked the managing director, "Why did your firm reduce the annual cohort frequency of Surge from two to one?"

**Options:**
(A) The journalist asked the managing director why did his firm reduce the annual cohort frequency of Surge from two to one. 
(B) The journalist asked the managing director why his firm had reduced the annual cohort frequency of Surge from two to one. 
(C) The journalist asked the managing director that why his firm had reduced the annual cohort frequency of Surge from two to one. 
(D) The journalist asked the managing director why had his firm reduced the annual cohort frequency of Surge from two to one. 
(E) The journalist asked the managing director why his firm reduced the annual cohort frequency of Surge from two to one. 

**Correct - B**
**Reasoning:**
When converting a 'wh-' interrogative sentence to indirect speech: (1) the question word 'why' acts as the joiner without 'that' (making C wrong); (2) the sentence structure changes from interrogative (verb-subject) to assertive (subject-verb), making A and D wrong; (3) the simple past 'did reduce' backshifts to the past perfect 'had reduced' (making E wrong). Option B complies with all these rules.

*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The advisor told the founders, "Do not accept this term sheet without analyzing its long-term equity dilution implications."

**Options:**
(A) The advisor advised the founders to not accept that term sheet without analyzing its long-term equity dilution implications. 
(B) The advisor advised the founders not to accept this term sheet without analyzing its long-term equity dilution implications. 
(C) The advisor advised the founders not to accept that term sheet without analyzing its long-term equity dilution implications. 
(D) The advisor advised the founders that they should not accept that term sheet without analyzing its long-term equity dilution implications. 
(E) The advisor advised the founders not to accept those term sheet without analyzing its long-term equity dilution implications. 

**Correct - C**
**Reasoning:**
In indirect speech, the negative imperative 'Do not accept' transforms into 'not to accept'. Additionally, the demonstrative adjective 'this' must change to 'that'. Option C correctly handles both transformations. Option A uses split-infinitive 'to not accept', Option B fails to change 'this', and Option E uses plural 'those' for singular 'term sheet'.

#### Q44. Narration — Modal "Must" Retention
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The senior partner observed, "Every early-stage venture capital firm must maintain strict financial discipline in a high-interest rate environment."

**Options:**
(A) The senior partner observed that every early-stage venture capital firm must maintain strict financial discipline in a high-interest rate environment. 
(B) The senior partner observed that every early-stage venture capital firm had to maintain strict financial discipline in a high-interest rate environment. 
(C) The senior partner observed that every early-stage venture capital firm must have maintained strict financial discipline in a high-interest rate environment. 
(D) The senior partner observed that every early-stage venture capital firm should maintain strict financial discipline in a high-interest rate environment. 
(E) The senior partner observed that every early-stage venture capital firm would maintain strict financial discipline in a high-interest rate environment. 

**Correct - A**
**Reasoning:**
The modal verb 'must' remains unchanged in indirect speech when it expresses a permanent rule, continuous duty, or universal truth that remains valid at the time of reporting. Since maintaining financial discipline in a high-interest rate environment is a continuous principle, 'must' is retained. Changing it to 'had to' would imply a past, completed obligation. Thus, Option A is the correct choice.

#### Q45. Narration — Exclamatory Sentence
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
"What a massive strategic pivot Peak XV is initiating with this sudden restructuring of Surge!" exclaimed the market analyst.

**Options:**
(A) The market analyst exclaimed with surprise what a massive strategic pivot Peak XV was initiating with that sudden restructuring of Surge. 
(B) The market analyst exclaimed that Peak XV was initiating a very massive strategic pivot with that sudden restructuring of Surge. 
(C) The market analyst exclaimed with surprise that Peak XV is initiating a very massive strategic pivot with this sudden restructuring of Surge. 
(D) The market analyst exclaimed with disgust that Peak XV had initiated a very massive strategic pivot with that sudden restructuring of Surge. 
(E) The market analyst exclaimed with joy that Peak XV was initiating a very massive strategic pivot with this sudden restructuring of Surge. 

**Correct - B**
**Reasoning:**
To convert an exclamatory sentence starting with 'What a...' into indirect speech: (1) change the structure into an assertive one using intensifiers like 'very' or 'greatly'; (2) change 'this' to 'that'; (3) backshift 'is initiating' to 'was initiating'; (4) remove the exclamation mark. Option B correctly changes 'What a massive pivot... is initiating with this' to 'that Peak XV was initiating a very massive pivot... with that'. Option A retains the exclamatory 'what a' order, and Option C fails to backshift.

### Q4. What is the best strategy to solve Active & Passive Voice in bank exams?

**Answer:** Active & Passive Voice transformations are a high-scoring topic in IBPS PO and SBI PO Mains. Follow this **5-step strategy**:

1. **Identify the Main Agent and Action:** Focus on finding the main subject, transitive verb, and direct object first. In complex sentences, do not confuse introductory phrases or trailing relative clauses with the main active clause.
2. **Handle Multi-Verb Auxiliary Chains with Precision:** For perfect modals (e.g., *ought to have*, *should have*), ensure you include 'been' in the passive form (*ought to have been raised*). Missing 'been' is a very common trap in Mains-level options.
3. **Check the Causative Verbs:** Remember that causative verbs like *make* take a bare infinitive in the active voice (*made them attend*) but require a full to-infinitive in the passive voice (*were made to attend*).
4. **Isolate Relative Clauses:** If the active sentence contains an embedded relative clause starting with *who/which/that*, check if its transformation is logically necessary. In most cases, only the main clause changes voice; transforming the relative clause unnecessarily often creates awkward phrasing or errors.
5. **Reverse the Agent Correctly:** In passive-to-active conversions, carefully identify the logical 'doer' from the *by*-phrase and set it as the singular or plural subject of the active sentence, ensuring that tense consistency is flawlessly maintained.
