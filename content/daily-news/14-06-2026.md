#### Q7. Spotting the Error — Subjunctive Mood in Coordinated Verbs
*Section: Advanced Spotting the Error — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The co-writer strongly proposed that the director (I) / completely overhauls the outdated second act of the script (II) / and focus on women dealing with a life without men. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Both (I) and (II) 
(B) Both (II) and (III) 
(C) Only (II) 
(D) Only (III) 
(E) All (I), (II), & (III)



**Correct - C**
**Reasoning:**
The verb 'proposed' followed by 'that' in part (I) triggers the mandative subjunctive mood in the succeeding noun clause. The subjunctive mood requires the base form of the verb, regardless of the singular subject ('the director'). Therefore, 'overhauls' in part (II) must be changed to 'overhaul'. Part (III) correctly utilizes the base verb 'focus' ('director... focus...').

#### Q10. Spotting the Error — Dangling Modifiers
*Section: Advanced Spotting the Error — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

#### Q41. Narration — Assertive Dual Tense Backshift
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
Suresh Triveni remarked, "Our customized acting workshops were highly successful and have established a great creative chemistry among the three actresses here."

**Options:**
(A) Suresh Triveni remarked that their customized acting workshops were highly successful and had established a great creative chemistry among the three actresses there.

(E) Suresh Triveni remarked that their customized acting workshops were highly successful and has established a great creative chemistry among the three actresses here. 

**Correct - B**
**Reasoning:**
Because the reporting verb is in the past tense ('remarked'), all tenses, pronouns, and adverbs in the direct speech must undergo backshift: (1) pronoun 'Our' changes to 'their'; (2) simple past verb 'were' backshifts to past perfect 'had been'; (3) present perfect 'have established' backshifts to past perfect 'had established'; (4) adverb 'here' shifts to 'there'. Option B correctly applies all these transformations.

#### Q42. Narration — Interrogative Wh-Question with Assertive Reorganization
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The journalist asked the writer-director, "Why did you choose Madhuri Dixit when you finalized the script for this unconventional film yesterday?"

**Options:**
(A) The journalist asked the writer-director why did he choose Madhuri Dixit when he finalized the script for this unconventional film yesterday. 
(B) The journalist asked the writer-director why he had chosen Madhuri Dixit when he had finalized the script for that unconventional film the previous day. 
(C) The journalist asked the writer-director that why he had chosen Madhuri Dixit when he had finalized the script for that unconventional film the previous day. 
(D) The journalist asked the writer-director why had he chosen Madhuri Dixit when he had finalized the script for that unconventional film the previous day. 
(E) The journalist asked the writer-director why he chose Madhuri Dixit when he watched the script for that unconventional film the previous day. 

**Correct - B**
**Reasoning:**
In indirect questions: (1) 'why' acts as the conjunction, and adding 'that' before it is ungrammatical (making C incorrect); (2) the sentence structure must change from interrogative ('why did you...') to assertive word order ('why he had chosen...', making A and D incorrect); (3) past tenses 'did choose' and 'finalized' backshift to past perfect 'had chosen' and 'had finalized'; (4) pronouns shift ('you' -> 'he'); (5) 'yesterday' shifts to 'the previous day' (making E incorrect). Option B is correct.

#### Q43. Narration — Negative Imperative with Demonstrative Shifts
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**

#### Q44. Narration — Modal "Must" Retention
*Section: Direct & Indirect Speech — IBPS PO / SSC CGL Mains Level*

**Question:**
The filmmaker observed, "Every modern work of art must initiate a passionate public conversation to achieve true cultural relevance in our society."

**Options:**
(A) The filmmaker observed that every modern work of art must initiate a passionate public conversation to achieve true cultural relevance in their society. 
(B) The filmmaker observed that every modern work of art had to initiate a passionate public conversation to achieve true cultural relevance in their society. 
(C) The filmmaker observed that every modern work of art must have initiated a passionate public conversation to achieve true cultural relevance in their society. 
(D) The filmmaker observed that every modern work of art should initiate a passionate public conversation to achieve true cultural relevance in their society. 
(E) The filmmaker observed that every modern work of art would initiate a passionate public conversation to achieve true cultural relevance in their society. 

**Correct - A**
**Reasoning:**
The modal auxiliary 'must' remains unchanged in indirect speech when it expresses a permanent rule, continuous duty, strong advice, or universal necessity that remains fully valid at the time of reporting. Since initiating a conversation is a permanent requirement for achieving cultural relevance, 'must' is retained. Changing it to 'had to' (Option B) would incorrectly imply a past, completed, one-time obligation. Option A is correct.

**Question:**
The co-writer exclaimed, "What a brilliant and uninhibited performance Dharna Durga has delivered in her debut film today!"

**Options:**
(A) The co-writer exclaimed what a brilliant and uninhibited performance Dharna Durga had delivered in her debut film that day.

### Q4. What is the best strategy to solve Active & Passive Voice in bank exams?

**Answer:** Active & Passive Voice is a high-scoring topic in IBPS PO and SBI PO Mains if approached systematically. Follow this **5-step strategy**:

1. **Verify Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) Alignment:** Identify the logical subject, main transitive verb, and direct object in the active sentence. Ensure that the object becomes the subject in the passive version, and the active subject becomes the agent preceded by 'by'.
2. **Track Complex Auxiliary Verb Chains:** Pay close attention to perfect and continuous aspect shifts. For perfect modals (e.g., *ought to have*), the passive voice must include 'been' (*ought to have been V3*). For continuous tenses, ensure 'being' is retained (*is being V3*).
3. **Handle Causative Verbs with Precision:** Remember that causative verbs like *make* take a bare infinitive in the active voice (*made the spectators jump*) but require a full to-infinitive in the passive voice (*were made to jump*).
4. **Isolate Embedded Relative Clauses:** If the active sentence contains an embedded relative clause (e.g., *that depicts...*), do not transform its voice unless it is logically necessary. Focus on transforming only the main verb of the principal clause.
5. **Reconcile Plural Agreements Post-Transformation:** When shifting from active to passive, the new passive subject may differ in number from the original active subject. Always check and adjust the helping verbs (*is/are*, *has/have*, *was/were*) to match the number of the new subject.
