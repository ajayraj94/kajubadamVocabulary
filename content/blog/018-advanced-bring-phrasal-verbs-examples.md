# Advanced 'Bring' Phrasal Verbs with English-Hindi Examples

In premium competitive examinations like **SSC CGL Tier 2**, **IBPS PO**, and **SBI PO Mains**, examiners frequently move beyond basic vocabulary to test highly sophisticated phrasal verbs. While basic "Bring" variants like *bring up* (raising a child) are well-known, advanced forms like **"bring off"** (succeeding in a difficult task), **"bring under"** (subduing a crisis), and **"bring through"** (guiding someone through a critical illness) are highly tested .

A minor contextual shift can completely alter the grammatical behavior and semantic meaning of these verbs. For instance, confusing "bring forward" (moving an event to an earlier date) with "put off" (postponing) is a common error in cloze tests. ## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: Temporal Shift Dynamics — "Bring forward" vs. "Put off"
In sentence correction and cloze test sections, candidates frequently confuse the temporal movements associated with scheduling phrasal verbs.

*   **The Difference:**
    1.  **Bring forward (Temporal Acceleration):** Means to reschedule an event to an **earlier** date or time than originally planned.
        *   *Example:* The meeting was **brought forward** from Friday to Wednesday. (यहाँ बैठक समय से पहले की गई).
    2.  **Put off / Postpone (Temporal Delay):** Means to reschedule an event to a **later** date or time.
        *   *Example:* The meeting was **put off** from Wednesday to Friday. (यहाँ बैठक आगे टाली गई).
*   **The Trap:** In exams, look out for sentences like "We need to bring forward the meeting because we need more time to prepare." This is contextually illogical because bringing a meeting forward *reduces* preparation time. The correct verb would be "put off."

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Transitivity and Separation Rules of "Bring off"
Understanding how the pronoun object is placed within **"bring off"** is a critical requirement for error detection.

*   **Rule:** "Bring off" (meaning to succeed in a difficult task) is a transitive separable phrasal verb. When the object of "bring off" is a **pronoun** (such as *it*, *them*, *him*, *her*), the pronoun must be placed between the verb and the particle. Placing it at the end is a major grammatical error.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** It was an extremely complex merger, but the corporate lawyer brought off it successfully.
*   ✅ **Correct:** It was an extremely complex merger, but the corporate lawyer **brought it off** successfully.

#### Q1. Fill in the Blank — Temporal Shift Context
**Question:** Since the major stakeholders are traveling abroad next Friday, the chairman decided to ________ the annual budget review to this Tuesday.

**Options:**
(A) put off
(B) bring forward
(C) bring up
(D) put down
(E) bring down

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The stakeholders are traveling next Friday, so the meeting must be held earlier. The phrasal verb "bring forward" means to move an event to an earlier date, which fits the context. "Put off" means to postpone to a later date, which is contextually incorrect.
- **Hindi:** हितधारक (stakeholders) अगले शुक्रवार को विदेश यात्रा पर जा रहे हैं, इसलिए बैठक पहले आयोजित करनी होगी। किसी कार्यक्रम या बैठक को पहले के समय पर तय करने के लिए सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"bring forward"** होता है। "Put off" का अर्थ टालना होता है, जो यहाँ संदर्भ के विपरीत है।

#### Q3. Spot the Error — Pronoun Placement with "Bring off"
**Question:** Securing a top rank in the civil services exam is tough, (I) / but the dedicated candidate from the village (II) / brought off it in his very first attempt. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (III) of the sentence. "Bring off" (meaning to achieve success in a difficult task) is a transitive separable phrasal verb. When the object is a pronoun (here, "it" referring to securing a rank), the pronoun must be placed in the middle. Therefore, "brought off it" must be corrected to **"brought it off."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। "Bring off" (कठिन कार्य सफलतापूर्वक पूरा करना) एक separable (विभाजित होने वाला) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। जब इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई सर्वनाम होता है (यहाँ रैंक सुरक्षित करने के लिए "it"), तो उस सर्वनाम को हमेशा वर्ब और पार्टिकल के बीच में रखा जाना चाहिए। इसलिए, "brought off it" के स्थान पर **"brought it off"** होना चाहिए।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Bring off" और "Bring about" के प्रयोग में क्या बुनियादी अंतर है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों के बीच अर्थ का बहुत स्पष्ट अंतर है:
1. **Bring off:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी अत्यधिक कठिन, चुनौतीपूर्ण या असंभव कार्य को सफलतापूर्वक पूरा करना (जैसे: *The lawyer managed to bring off the difficult case*).
2. **Bring about:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी बड़े बदलाव, घटना या परिस्थिति का कारण बनना (जैसे: *The new rules brought about massive changes*).

### Q2. "Bring forward" और "Put off" समय परिवर्तन में कैसे भिन्न हैं?
**Answer:** यह परीक्षाओं का एक बहुत ही पसंदीदा विपरीतार्थक युग्म (antonym pair) है:
* **Bring forward:** इसका अर्थ है किसी निर्धारित बैठक या कार्यक्रम को पहले के समय पर आयोजित करना (जैसे शुक्रवार की जगह बुधवार को करना)।
* **Put off:** इसका अर्थ है किसी कार्यक्रम को आगे की तारीख के लिए टालना या स्थगित करना (जैसे बुधवार की जगह शुक्रवार को करना)।

### Q3. "Bring through" का उपयोग किस प्रकार की परिस्थितियों में किया जाता है?
**Answer:** "Bring through" का प्रयोग मुख्य रूप से किसी व्यक्ति (विशेष रूप से मरीज) को किसी बहुत ही गंभीर बीमारी, जानलेवा दुर्घटना, या अत्यंत कठिन संकट से सुरक्षित बाहर निकालने और उसे बचाने के संदर्भ में किया जाता है (जैसे: *The doctors managed to bring the patient through*).
