# Confusing Phrasal Verbs: Come across vs Come by vs Come up with with Sentences & Hindi Meanings

Phrasal verbs originating from the base verb **"Come"** are highly favored by examiners in competitive exams like **SSC CGL, SBI PO, IBPS PO, and UPSC CDS** [1]. Among these, **"Come across," "Come by,"** and **"Come up with"** are particularly confusing because they often appear in similar contexts but convey entirely different actions [1]. 

For example, do you *come across* an opportunity, *come by* a rare asset, or *come up with* a solution? A minor prepositional shift changes the sentence from expressing an accidental discovery (*come across*) to a hard-earned acquisition (*come by*) or the cognitive act of inventing an idea (*come up with*).

To build permanent semantic clarity and prevent errors under exam pressure, the academic editorial team at **kajubadamvocabulary.in** has compiled this comprehensive guide. Through a bilingual mnemonic story, comparative tables, and highly detailed practice questions, we will clarify these confusing phrasal verbs.

प्रेरणा के लिए पुराने अभिलेखागार (archives) में खोज करते समय, उन्हें अचानक एक डिजिटल रणनीति का भुला दिया गया मसौदा **मिला (came across)**।

As the presentation slides were coming along nicely, a major roadblock hit the team.  
जैसे-जैसे प्रेजेंटेशन स्लाइड्स अच्छे से **तैयार हो रही थीं/आगे बढ़ रही थीं (coming along)**, टीम को एक बड़ी बाधा का सामना करना पड़ा।

Despite the setback, Amit delivered the pitch so brilliantly that the skeptical board members quickly came around to approve the budget.

## 📝 The Core Vocabulary Masterclass — मुख्य शब्दकोश तालिका

This comparative table highlights the syntactic differences, precise meanings, and exam traps of these closely related phrasal verbs [1].

| Phrasal Verb | English Meaning | Hindi Meaning | Exam-Style Example | Grammar Trap / Key Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Come across** | To find or meet someone or something by chance. | अचानक मिलना या संयोग से मिल जाना। | While cleaning the old drawer, she **came across** a rare colonial-era coin. | Redundancy trap: Do not use "suddenly" or "with" with it. |
| **Come by** | To obtain or acquire something, especially when difficult. | प्रयास से प्राप्त करना या मिलना। | High-paying corporate jobs are extremely hard to **come by** during a recession. | Transitive; do not confuse with "come around" or "come up." |
| **Come up with** | To suggest, produce, or invent an idea, plan, or solution. | कोई विचार, योजना या उपाय सोचना या तैयार करना। | The research department was asked to **come up with** a sustainable alternative to plastic. | Three-word inseparable phrasal verb. Requires 'up' and 'with' together. |
| **Come off** | To succeed; or to take place; or to become unfastened/detached. | सफल होना; घटित होना; या किसी चीज़ का अलग हो जाना (जैसे बटन)। | The grand launch event **came off** beautifully despite the rain. | Polysemous; carefully distinguish the "success" meaning from "detaching." |
| **Come along** | To make progress or improve; or to arrive or accompany. | प्रगति करना या सुधार होना; साथ आना या पहुँचना। | The construction of the highway is **coming along** quite smoothly. | Often used to ask about the progress of a task or recovery. |
| **Come about** | To happen, occur, or take place (especially unexpectedly). | घटित होना या कोई परिस्थिति उत्पन्न होना। | Can you explain to the board how this financial deficit **came about**? | Intransitive; synonym of "happen." Do not use in passive voice. |
| **Come around** | To change one's opinion or agree; or regain consciousness. | अपनी राय बदलना या सहमत होना; अथवा होश में आना। | After a long discussion, they finally **came around** to our proposal. | Also written as "come round." Intransitive. |
| **Come down with** | To contract or fall ill with a minor disease or sickness. | किसी बीमारी या बुखार की चपेट में आना/बीमार पड़ना। | Many students in the hostel have **come down with** viral fever this week. | Strictly transitive; must be followed directly by the name of the illness. |

## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: Semantic Divergence: "Come across" (Accidental) vs. "Come by" (Acquisition)
Candidates often struggle to choose between **"come across"** and **"come by"** in precise fill-in-the-blank questions.

*   **The Key Distinction:**
    1.  **Come across (Accidental/Passive):** Refers to finding or meeting someone or something purely by chance, without any prior intention or effort.
        *   *Example:* While organizing the library, I **came across** an ancient diary. (Correct)
    2.  **Come by (Deliberate/Active Acquisition):** Refers to obtaining or acquiring something that is rare, highly valuable, or requires significant effort or luck to secure.
        *   *Example:* How did you **come by** such a rare and expensive painting? (Correct)
        *   ❌ *Incorrect:* How did you come across such a rare and expensive painting? (While grammatically possible if found by chance, if the context implies active ownership or acquisition, "come by" is the precise idiomatic choice).

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: The Double Meaning of "Come off" (Succeeding vs. Detaching)
The phrasal verb **"come off"** is highly polysemous, making it a favorite for advanced reading comprehension and cloze tests.

1.  **Succeeding (Intransitive):** When used to describe a plan, scheme, or event, it means to be successful or yield the desired result.
    *   *Example:* The manager’s daring plan to capture the market **came off** perfectly. (Correct)
2.  **Detaching (Intransitive):** When used to describe a physical part of an object (like a button, handle, or wheel), it means to become unfastened or separated.
    *   *Example:* The front wheel of the bicycle suddenly **came off** while he was riding down the hill. (Correct)

#### Q1. Fill in the Blank — Hard-to-Get Acquisition
**Question:** During the severe economic downturn, well-paying freelance opportunities in the tech sector were extremely hard to ________.

**Options:**
(A) come across with
(B) come by
(C) come up with
(D) come along
(E) come about

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The sentence describes well-paying opportunities being difficult to obtain or acquire. The phrasal verb "come by" means to obtain or acquire something that is rare or difficult to get, which fits this context perfectly. "Come across with" is ungrammatical, and "come up with" means to invent an idea, which does not fit.
- **Hindi:** वाक्य में आर्थिक मंदी के दौरान अच्छे भुगतान वाले फ्रीलांस अवसरों को "प्राप्त करने या पाने" की कठिनाई का संदर्भ है। किसी दुर्लभ या कठिन अवसर को प्राप्त करने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"come by"** होता है। "Come across with" व्याकरणिक रूप से अशुद्ध है, अतः विकल्प (B) सही उत्तर है।

#### Q2. Spot the Error — Redundant Modifier with Accident Discovery
**Question:** While sorting out (I) / the ancient coin collection in the family archive, (II) / Nilesh suddenly came across with a rare Greek drachma. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). The phrasal verb "come across" means "to meet or find by chance/unexpectedly". Adding "suddenly" and "with" is grammatically redundant and incorrect. The correct construction should be "he **came across** a rare Greek drachma."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। "Come across" का अर्थ ही "अचानक या संयोग से मिलना" होता है। इसके साथ "suddenly" का प्रयोग करना एक पुनरुक्ति दोष (redundancy) है, और इसके बाद "with" लगाना व्याकरण के नियमों के विरुद्ध है। इसे शुद्ध करने के लिए "suddenly" और "with" दोनों को हटा दिया जाना चाहिए।

#### Q3. Sentence Improvement — Success Context
**Question:** We were worried about the complex stage design, but the entire theatrical performance **came out** beautifully.

**Options:**
(A) came off
(B) came up with
(C) came along
(D) came around
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The sentence refers to the theatrical performance succeeding or taking place beautifully. The phrasal verb "come off" means to be successful or succeed, which fits the context of a performance turning out well. "Come out" means to be published, which is incorrect here. Therefore, option (A) is the correct improvement.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ थिएटर प्रदर्शन के खूबसूरती से "सफल होने या संपन्न होने" का संदर्भ है। किसी योजना या प्रदर्शन के सफल होने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"come off"** होता है। "Come out" का अर्थ प्रकाशित होना होता है, जो यहाँ असंगत है। अतः विकल्प (A) सही सुधार है।

#### Q5. Spot the Error — Incorrect Preposition with Disease Contraction
**Question:** The chief technology officer was forced (I) / to cancel his keynote address (II) / because he had come down of a high fever. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). The phrasal verb meaning to fall ill with a sickness is "come down with." Using the preposition "of" instead of "with" is grammatically incorrect. The sentence must be corrected to "because he had **come down with** a high fever."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। किसी बीमारी या बुखार से पीड़ित होने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब "come down with" होता है। यहाँ "with" के स्थान पर "of" का प्रयोग करना व्याकरण की दृष्टि से पूरी तरह अशुद्ध है। इसे सुधारकर **"come down with a high fever"** किया जाना चाहिए।

#### Q7. Sentence Improvement — Regaining Consciousness
**Question:** The patient was heavily sedated after the surgery, but the doctor expected him to **come along** within a few hours.

**Options:**
(A) come around
(B) come down with
(C) come by
(D) come off
(E) No correction required

- **Hindi:** यहाँ सर्जरी के बाद भारी बेहोशी (sedation) से रोगी के कुछ घंटों में "होश में आने" की उम्मीद की जा रही है। बेहोशी के बाद होश में आने के लिए सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"come around"** (या *come round*) होता है। "Come along" का अर्थ प्रगति करना होता है, इसलिए विकल्प (A) सही सुधार है।

#### Q10. Comprehensive Syntax Check — Multiple Choice Pattern (SBI PO Mains Level)
**Question:** Identify the grammatically correct sentence(s) from the options provided below:
(I) A reliable and trustworthy business partner is exceptionally difficult to come by these days.
(II) The startup's launch came off beautifully, attracting millions in venture capital.
(III) The handle of the heavy suitcase came off when I tried to lift it.

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Both (I) and (III)
(D) All (I), (II), and (III)
(E) None of the sentences are correct

**Correct Answer - D**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** All three sentences are grammatically and syntactically correct. Sentence (I) correctly uses "come by" to mean obtaining or acquiring a rare business partner. Sentence (II) correctly uses "came off" to mean succeeded. Sentence (III) correctly uses "came off" to mean becoming detached or unfastened physically.
- **Hindi:** दिए गए तीनों वाक्य पूरी तरह से शुद्ध हैं। वाक्य (I) में दुर्लभ व्यावसायिक भागीदार को "प्राप्त करने" के अर्थ में "come by" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (II) में भव्य लॉन्च के "सफल होने" के अर्थ में "came off" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (III) में सूटकेस के हैंडल के "अलग हो जाने" के अर्थ में "came off" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। अतः विकल्प (D) सही उत्तर है।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Come across" और "Come by" के बीच मुख्य व्यावहारिक अंतर क्या है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों के बीच का अंतर उनके प्राप्ति के तरीके में होता है:
*   **Come across:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी व्यक्ति या वस्तु का बिना किसी पूर्व प्रयास के, अचानक या संयोग से मिल जाना (जैसे: *I came across an old photo*).
*   **Come by:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी ऐसी वस्तु को कठिन प्रयासों या भाग्य से प्राप्त करना जो बहुत दुर्लभ या मूल्यवान हो (जैसे: *A good job is hard to come by*).

### Q2. "Come up with" का प्रयोग किस प्रकार के संदर्भों में किया जाता है?
**Answer:** "Come up with" का प्रयोग मुख्य रूप से मानसिक या रचनात्मक कार्यों के लिए किया जाता है, जहाँ आपको कोई नया विचार, योजना, रणनीति, बहाना या किसी समस्या का समाधान तैयार करना या सोचना होता है (जैसे: *She came up with a brilliant idea*).

### Q3. "Come off" का प्रयोग "सफल होने" के अलावा और किस अर्थ में होता है?
**Answer:** "Come off" का एक अन्य बहुत ही सामान्य अर्थ होता है **"किसी वस्तु का मुख्य भाग से अलग हो जाना या टूट जाना"** (to become unfastened or detached)। जैसे किसी शर्ट का बटन टूट जाना या दरवाजे का हैंडल अलग हो जाना (e.g., *The button came off my shirt*).
