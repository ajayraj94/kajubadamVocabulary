## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: The Lexical Ambiguity of "Pass out" — Clinical vs. Academic Contexts
In vocabulary and sentence completion sections, examiners often exploit the two completely distinct meanings of **"Pass out."**

*   **The Rule:** 
    1.  **Clinical Sense (Fainting):** To lose consciousness. It is strictly intransitive and usually followed by prepositions like *from* or *due to*.
        *   *Example:* He **passed out** from extreme heat during the parade.
    2.  **Academic/Service Sense (Graduating):** To successfully complete a course of study, particularly at a military, police, or air force academy. This meaning is also intransitive and is usually followed by *of* or *from*.
        *   *Example:* The newly commissioned officers will **pass out** of the academy tomorrow.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Transitive Separability Rules — "Pass over" vs. "Pass on"
Understanding the transitivity and separability of these verbs is essential for answering sentence structure questions correctly.

*   **The Rule:** 
    1.  **Pass over** (meaning to ignore or bypass someone for a promotion) is **transitive and separable**. If the object is a personal pronoun, it must be placed between "pass" and "over."
        *   ❌ **Incorrect:** The committee decided to pass over him because of his poor performance.
        *   ✅ **Correct:** The committee decided to **pass him over** because of his poor performance.
    2.  **Pass on** (meaning to transmit or give something) is **transitive and separable** when followed by an object. However, when used as a polite euphemism for dying (synonymous with *pass away*), it is strictly **intransitive**.
        *   *Example (Transitive):* Please **pass the details on** to the administrative team.
        *   *Example (Intransitive):* The patient peacefully **passed on** in his sleep.

#### Q1. Spot the Error — Semantic Word Swap Trap (SSC CGL Tier-2 Level)
**Question:** During the intensive physical drills (I) / in the humid summer afternoon, (II) / several young soldiers passed down due to heat strokes. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (III). The phrasal verb meaning to lose consciousness or faint is "pass out," not "pass down." "Pass down" means to transmit traditions or legacies through generations. Therefore, "passed down" must be replaced with "passed out" to restore the correct meaning of the sentence.
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। भीषण गर्मी में चक्कर आने या बेहोश होने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब "pass out" (भूतकाल "passed out") होता है, न कि "pass down"। "Pass down" का अर्थ विरासत या परंपरा को आगे बढ़ाना होता है, जो यहाँ असंगत है। अतः वाक्य को शुद्ध करने के लिए "passed down" के स्थान पर "passed out" का प्रयोग होना चाहिए।

#### Q2. Fill in the Blank — Double Concept Matching (Bank PO Level)
**Question:** The royal family was plunged into mourning when the queen ________, but they were pleased when the crown prince ________ from the defense college.

**Options:**
(A) passed off / passed over
(B) passed away / passed out
(C) passed out / passed away
(D) passed down / passed up
(E) passed up / passed on

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The first blank describes the queen's death, which is represented by the phrasal verb "passed away." The second blank describes the crown prince graduating from the defense college, which is represented by the phrasal verb "passed out." This makes option (B) the only grammatically and logically correct choice.
- **Hindi:** पहले रिक्त स्थान में रानी की मृत्यु को दर्शाने के लिए "passed away" की आवश्यकता है। दूसरे रिक्त स्थान में युवराज के डिफेंस कॉलेज से "दीक्षित होने/सफलतापूर्वक कोर्स पूरा करने" के अर्थ में "passed out" की आवश्यकता है। अतः विकल्प (B) सही और तर्कसंगत उत्तर है।

#### Q3. Sentence Improvement — Bypassed Candidate Context (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** The senior engineer chose to resign from his job after he was **passed up** for the project lead position despite his outstanding experience.

**Options:**
(A) passed off
(B) passed over
(C) passed out
(D) passed down
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The sentence describes an engineer being overlooked or ignored for a promotion or project lead position. The correct phrasal verb meaning to ignore or bypass someone for a promotion is "pass over" (past tense "passed over"). "Passed up" means to decline an opportunity, which is incorrect because he did not decline it; he was bypassed. Thus, option (B) is the correct improvement.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ कनिष्ठ (junior) के पक्ष में वरिष्ठ इंजीनियर को पदोन्नति के लिए "नजरअंदाज किए जाने" का संदर्भ है। पदोन्नति या किसी जिम्मेदारी के लिए नजरअंदाज किए जाने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"pass over"** होता है। "Passed up" का अर्थ स्वयं किसी अवसर को अस्वीकार करना होता है। अतः विकल्प (B) सही सुधार है।

#### Q4. Phrase Replacement — Impersonation Context (NDA Level)
**Question:** The clever fraudster tried to **pass over** himself as a government official to gain unauthorized entry into the high-security zone.

**Options:**
(A) pass off
(B) pass out
(C) pass up
(D) pass down
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The sentence describes a fraudster pretending to be or misrepresenting himself as a government official. The correct phrasal verb meaning to pretend to be someone else or to misrepresent is "pass off" (specifically, "pass oneself off as"). "Pass over" means to ignore for a promotion, which does not fit. #### Q5. Spot the Error — Intransitive Passive Voice Trap (Bank PO Level)
**Question:** The former prime minister was passed away (I) / after suffering from a prolonged cardiovascular illness (II) / at the national research hospital. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (I). The phrasal verb "pass away" meaning to die is strictly intransitive. Since intransitive phrasal verbs cannot be used in the passive voice, using the auxiliary verb "was" before "passed away" is grammatically incorrect. The sentence should be written in the active past tense as "The former prime minister passed away..."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (I) में है। "Pass away" (मृत्यु होना) एक अकर्मक क्रिया (intransitive verb) है। अकर्मक क्रियाओं का पैसिव वॉइस नहीं बनाया जा सकता, इसलिए "passed away" के पहले सहायक क्रिया "was" का प्रयोग व्याकरणिक रूप से पूरी तरह गलत है। इसे सुधारकर एक्टिव वॉइस में केवल **"passed away"** लिखा जाना चाहिए। अतः विकल्प (A) सही उत्तर है।

#### Q10. Comprehensive Syntax Check — Multiple Statement Pattern (SBI PO Mains Level)
**Question:** Identify the grammatically correct sentence(s) from the options provided below:
(I) The cultural festival passed off smoothly despite the minor organizational delays.
(II) He staggered due to the scorching heat and nearly passed out on the parade ground.
(III) The committee decided to pass him over for the lead role due to his lack of commitment.

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Both (I) and (III)
(D) All (I), (II), and (III)
(E) None of the sentences are correct

**Correct Answer - D**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** All three sentences are grammatically and syntactically correct. Sentence (I) correctly uses "passed off" to mean took place or happened. Sentence (II) correctly uses "passed out" to mean fainted. Sentence (III) correctly uses "passed him over" to mean bypassed or ignored him for a position.
- **Hindi:** दिए गए तीनों वाक्य व्याकरण और संदर्भ की दृष्टि से पूरी तरह से शुद्ध हैं। वाक्य (I) में त्योहार के "संपन्न होने" के अर्थ में "passed off" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (II) में परेड ग्राउंड पर गर्मी में "बेहोश होने" के अर्थ में "passed out" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (III) में मुख्य भूमिका के लिए उम्मीदवार को "नजरअंदाज करने" के अर्थ में "passed him over" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। अतः विकल्प (D) सही उत्तर है।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Pass over" और "Pass up" के बीच मुख्य अंतर क्या है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों के बीच अंतर को उनके संदर्भ से समझा जा सकता है:
*   **Pass over:** इसका प्रयोग तब होता है जब किसी व्यक्ति को किसी नौकरी या पदोन्नति (promotion) के लिए **नजरअंदाज या बाईपास** कर दिया जाता है (जैसे: *He was passed over for the job*).
*   **Pass up:** इसका प्रयोग तब होता है जब आप स्वयं किसी अच्छे अवसर या प्रस्ताव को **अस्वीकार कर देते हैं या छोड़ देते हैं** (जैसे: *Never pass up a good opportunity*).

### Q2. क्या "Pass out" का प्रयोग पैसिव वॉइस (Passive Voice) में किया जा सकता है?
**Answer:** नहीं, "Pass out" (बेहोश होना या दीक्षित होना) एक अकर्मक क्रिया (intransitive phrasal verb) है। चूंकि अकर्मक क्रियाओं का कोई प्रत्यक्ष कर्म (direct object) नहीं होता, इसलिए व्याकरण के नियमों के अनुसार इनका पैसिव वॉइस बनाना पूरी तरह अशुद्ध माना जाता है।

### Q3. "He tried to pass over himself as a doctor" लिखना गलत क्यों है?
**Answer:** यहाँ "डॉक्टर के रूप में खुद को गलत तरीके से पेश करने या ढोंग करने" का संदर्भ है। इस विशिष्ट अर्थ के लिए सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"pass off"** (pass oneself off as) होता है, न कि "pass over"। अतः सही वाक्य **"He tried to pass himself off as a doctor"** होगा।
