## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: Double Preposition Dynamics and Strict Inseparability
In advanced sentence correction sections, candidates are often tested on the syntax of three-word phrasal verbs containing **"Come"** (such as *come up with* and *come down with*).

*   **The Rule:** These phrasal verbs consist of a transitive verb, an adverbial particle, and a preposition. Because they are strictly **inseparable**, you cannot place any direct object between the verb and the prepositions. Furthermore, dropping either of the two prepositions (using *come up* instead of *come up with* in a transitive context) constitutes a severe grammatical error.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The committee spent several days discussing the crisis and finally *came up a brilliant plan*.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The committee spent several days discussing the crisis and finally **came up with** a brilliant plan.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: The Linking Verb Trap of "Come out" vs. "Publish"
In error-spotting questions, examiners frequently exploit the differences between the transitive verb **"publish"** and the intransitive phrasal verb **"come out."**

*   **The Rule:** 
    1.  **Publish** is a transitive verb. It requires an object and can be used in the passive voice.
        *   *Example:* The historic novel **was published** by the press last year. (Correct)
    2.  **Come out** is an intransitive phrasal verb meaning "to be published or released." Because it is intransitive, it **cannot** have a passive voice construction (using *was/were came out* is incorrect).
        *   ❌ **Incorrect:** The scientific journal *was came out* last Monday.
        *   ✅ **Correct:** The scientific journal **came out** last Monday.

#### Q1. Spot the Error — Intransitive Phrasal Verb Trap (SSC CGL Tier-2 Level)
**Question:** The comprehensive investigative report (I) / on recent environmental hazards (II) / was came out last week to shock the public. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). The phrasal verb "come out" means "to be published or released." As an intransitive phrasal verb, it cannot be written in the passive voice. Therefore, using the auxiliary verb "was" before "came out" is grammatically incorrect. The correct construction is simply "came out last week."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। "Come out" का अर्थ "प्रकाशित होना" होता है और यह एक अकर्मक क्रिया (intransitive verb) है। अकर्मक क्रियाओं का पैसिव वॉइस (passive voice) नहीं बनाया जा सकता, इसलिए यहाँ सहायक क्रिया "was" का प्रयोग सर्वथा अनुचित है। इसे सुधारकर केवल **"came out last week"** लिखा जाना चाहिए।

#### Q2. Fill in the Blank — Double Preposition Matching (Bank PO Level)
**Question:** The research supervisor was pleased when the team was able to ________ a brilliant solution, but she was worried when the lead developer ________ a migraine.

**Options:**
(A) come along / came about
(B) come up with / came down with
(C) come by / came around
(D) come off / came down with
(E) come about / come up with

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The first blank requires a phrasal verb meaning to suggest or produce a solution, which is "come up with." The second blank requires a verb indicating that the lead developer fell ill with a migraine, which is "came down with." This makes option (B) the only logically and grammatically correct choice.
- **Hindi:** पहले रिक्त स्थान में समाधान "तैयार करने या खोजने" के अर्थ में "come up with" की आवश्यकता है। दूसरे रिक्त स्थान में डेवलपर के माइग्रेन (सिरदर्द) से "पीड़ित होने या बीमार पड़ने" के अर्थ में "came down with" की आवश्यकता है। अतः विकल्प (B) व्याकरण और संदर्भ दोनों की दृष्टि से सबसे उपयुक्त है।

#### Q3. Phrase Replacement — Redundancy Avoidance (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** While browsing through the crowded book fair, I **suddenly came across with** a rare first-edition copy of my favorite novel.

**Options:**
(A) came across
(B) suddenly came across
(C) came across with
(D) came up with
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The phrasal verb "come across" inherently means to find or meet someone or something by chance or unexpectedly. Adding the adverb "suddenly" or the preposition "with" is grammatically redundant. Therefore, removing both "suddenly" and "with" to use simply **"came across"** is the correct replacement.
- **Hindi:** "Come across" का अर्थ ही "अचानक या संयोग से मिलना" होता है। इसके साथ "suddenly" का प्रयोग करना एक पुनरुक्ति दोष (redundancy) है, और इसके बाद "with" लगाना पूरी तरह अशुद्ध है। अतः सही सुधार विकल्प (A) यानी केवल **"came across"** होगा।

#### Q5. Spot the Error — Semantic Misuse of Arriving vs. Regaining Consciousness
**Question:** The patient remained in a state of deep coma for six hours, (I) / but the chief neurosurgeon assured the anxious family (II) / that he would come along in the recovery ward. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). The context describes an unconscious patient regaining consciousness. The correct phrasal verb meaning to regain consciousness or wake up is "come around" (or *come round*). "Come along" means to make progress or improve, which is less precise in this clinical context. It should be changed to **"come around."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। यहाँ बेहोश मरीज के "होश में आने" का संदर्भ है। बेहोशी या कोमा के बाद होश में आने के लिए सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"come around"** (या *come round*) होता है। "Come along" का अर्थ प्रगति करना होता है, जो यहाँ अनुचित है। अतः विकल्प (C) सही उत्तर है।

#### Q8. Spot the Error — Separability Violation of Three-Word Phrasal Verbs (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** The lead programmer spent hours (I) / analyzing the server database and finally (II) / came up a brilliant security patch with. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). "Come up with" is a three-word inseparable phrasal verb. The direct object ("a brilliant security patch") cannot split the particles of the verb; it must be placed at the very end, directly after the preposition "with." The sentence should correctly read "**came up with** a brilliant security patch."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। "Come up with" तीन शब्दों का एक ऐसा फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है जिसे विभाजित नहीं किया जा सकता (inseparable)। इसका ऑब्जेक्ट ("a brilliant security patch") कभी भी वर्ब और इसके पार्टिकल्स के बीच में नहीं आ सकता। सही संरचना "**came up with** a brilliant security patch" होनी चाहिए।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. परीक्षा में "Come across" के साथ "With" का प्रयोग गलत क्यों माना जाता है?
**Answer:** "Come across" एक सकर्मक वाक्यांश क्रिया (transitive phrasal verb) है जो सीधे अपने ऑब्जेक्ट को लेती है। इसके बाद "with" का प्रयोग करना (जैसे: *I came across with my friend*) एक गंभीर व्याकरणिक त्रुटि है। सही रूप केवल **"come across [object]"** होता है।

### Q2. "Come out" और "Publish" के पैसिव वॉइस के नियम में क्या अंतर है?
**Answer:** "Publish" एक सकर्मक क्रिया (transitive verb) है, इसलिए इसका पैसिव वॉइस बनाया जा सकता है (जैसे: *The book was published*). इसके विपरीत, "Come out" एक अकर्मक क्रिया (intransitive verb) है, इसलिए इसका पैसिव वॉइस नहीं बनाया जा सकता (जैसे: *The book was came out* अशुद्ध है; केवल *The book came out* सही है)।

### Q3. "Come off" के दो विपरीत अर्थों को वाक्य में कैसे पहचानें?
**Answer:** "Come off" के दो अर्थ संदर्भ पर निर्भर करते हैं:
1. **सफल होना (To succeed):** जब इसका प्रयोग किसी योजना, प्रयास, या कार्यक्रम के लिए किया जाता है (जैसे: *The plan came off well*).
2. **अलग होना (To detach):** जब इसका प्रयोग किसी वस्तु के हिस्से (जैसे बटन, हैंडल) के टूटकर अलग होने के लिए किया जाता है (जैसे: *The button came off*).
