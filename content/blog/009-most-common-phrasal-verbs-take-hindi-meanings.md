# Most Common Phrasal Verbs with 'Take' with Hindi Meanings

In advanced competitive exams like **SSC CGL Tier 2**, **IBPS PO**, and **SBI PO Mains**, vocabulary questions are heavily focused on verbs that change meaning depending on their trailing prepositions. Among these high-yield verb families, **"Take"** is one of the most prolific. 

The phrasal verbs created with "Take"—such as *take after*, *take over*, and *take off*—carry multiple distinct meanings that are frequently tested in error spotting and cloze tests. For example, "take off" can refer to an aircraft leaving the ground, a person removing their clothes, or a business suddenly becoming highly successful. ## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: The passive voice trap of "Take in"
Examiners frequently target **"take in"** in its passive form to test reading comprehension and error spotting.

*   **The Rule:** When "take in" means "to deceive or cheat," it is almost always tested in the **passive voice**. Candidates often fail to recognize that "be + taken in" means "to be deceived" (धोखा खाना).
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The innocent villagers *took in* by the sweet talks of the traveling salesman.
*   The sentence lacks the auxiliary verb required for the passive voice.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The innocent villagers **were taken in** by the sweet talks of the traveling salesman.
*   This correctly indicates that the villagers were the victims of deception.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: The Gerund Constraint of "Take to"
One of the most heavily tested rules in bank exams (SBI/IBPS PO) involves the grammatical structure following **"take to"** (meaning to develop a habit or liking for).

*   **The Rule:** In the phrasal verb "take to," the word **"to"** functions strictly as a **preposition**, not as an infinitive marker. Therefore, any verb immediately following "take to" must be in the **gerund form (V-ing form)** rather than the base form of the verb.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** After experiencing severe stress at work, he unfortunately took to *drink* heavily.
*   "drink" is a base verb, which violates the prepositional constraint of "to".
*   ✅ **Correct:** After experiencing severe stress at work, he unfortunately took to **drinking** heavily.
*   "drinking" is a gerund, acting correctly as the object of the preposition.

#### Q1. Spot the Error — The Prepositional Gerund Trap
**Question:** After his retirement from the administrative service, (I) / the former officer took to spend (II) / most of his time volunteering at the local orphanage. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Both (I) and (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (II) of the sentence. The phrasal verb "took to" requires a gerund (V-ing form) because the word "to" functions as a preposition, not an infinitive marker. Therefore, the base verb "spend" must be changed to the gerund **"spending"** to make the sentence grammatically correct.
- **Hindi:** वाक्य के भाग (II) में त्रुटि है। "Took to" फ्रेज़ल वर्ब में "to" एक preposition की तरह कार्य करता है, इसलिए इसके बाद हमेशा gerund (V-ing रूप) का प्रयोग होना चाहिए। इस कारण क्रिया के मूल रूप "spend" के स्थान पर **"spending"** का प्रयोग किया जाना चाहिए।

#### Q4. Spot the Error — Pronoun Separation Constraint
**Question:** Before entering the sacred temple premises, (I) / the devotees were requested to take off them (II) / and leave their leather articles outside. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (II). "Take off" (meaning to remove clothing or footwear) is a transitive separable phrasal verb. When the object is a pronoun (here, "them" referring to shoes/leather articles), the pronoun must be placed between the verb and the particle. Therefore, "take off them" must be corrected to **"take them off."**
- **Hindi:** वाक्य के भाग (II) में त्रुटि है। "Take off" (कपड़े या जूते उतारना) एक transitive separable फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। जब इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई सर्वनाम होता है (यहाँ जूतों/चमड़े की वस्तुओं के लिए "them"), तो उस सर्वनाम को हमेशा वर्ब और प्रीपोजीशन के बीच में रखा जाना चाहिए। इसलिए, "take off them" के स्थान पर **"take them off"** होना चाहिए।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Take after" और "Look after" में परीक्षा की दृष्टि से क्या अंतर है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों में काफी बड़ा अंतर है और परीक्षा में यह अक्सर छात्रों को भ्रमित करने के लिए दिया जाता है:
1. **Take after:** इसका अर्थ होता है अपने परिवार के किसी सदस्य (जैसे माता-पिता या दादा-दादी) के जैसा दिखना या उनके जैसे स्वभाव का होना (जैसे: *She takes after her mother*).
2. **Look after:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी बीमार, बच्चे या वस्तु की देखभाल करना या उसकी जिम्मेदारी संभालना (जैसे: *Nurses look after the patients*).

### Q2. "Take to" के बाद क्रिया में 'ing' लगाने का व्याकरणिक नियम क्या है?
**Answer:** "Take to" (कोई आदत डालना या किसी को पसंद करने लगना) में आने वाला शब्द **"to" एक Preposition है**, न कि Infinitive का हिस्सा। अंग्रेजी व्याकरण के बुनियादी नियम के अनुसार, किसी भी Preposition के तुरंत बाद आने वाली क्रिया हमेशा **Gerund (V-ing form)** के रूप में होनी चाहिए। इसलिए परीक्षाओं में हमेशा *took to drinking/gambling* को सही माना जाता है, न कि *took to drink/gamble*।

### Q3. "Take on" के परीक्षा उपयोगी अलग-अलग अर्थ क्या हैं?
**Answer:** "Take on" के दो प्रमुख अर्थ परीक्षाओं में बहुत पूछे जाते हैं:
1. **जिम्मेदारी या चुनौती स्वीकार करना:** किसी कठिन कार्य या प्रोजेक्ट को अपने हाथ में लेना (जैसे: *He decided to take on the new project*).
2. **कर्मचारियों को काम पर रखना (Employ):** नए स्टाफ की नियुक्ति करना (जैसे: *The firm plans to take on ten new software developers*).
