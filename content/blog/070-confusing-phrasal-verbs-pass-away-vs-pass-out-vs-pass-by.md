# Confusing Phrasal Verbs: Pass away vs Pass out vs Pass by with Sentences & Hindi Meanings for Competitive Exams

In competitive exams like SSC CGL, SBI PO, IBPS PO, and UPSC CDS, examiners frequently construct questions using phrasal verbs that look very similar but carry entirely different meanings. Among these, phrasal verbs built around the verb **"Pass"**—specifically **"Pass away,"** **"Pass out,"** and **"Pass by"**—are highly confusing for candidates.

Making a mistake between a person "passing out" from heat exhaustion and an opportunity "passing by" can easily lead to a loss of marks in sentence improvement, error detection, or cloze tests. To help you build absolute conceptual clarity, the academic editorial team at **kajubadamvocabulary.in** has prepared this comprehensive bilingual guide. We will analyze the precise differences among these verbs using our contextual story method, clear grammar spotlights, and standard exam-style practice questions.

## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: Double Preposition Confusion — "Pass by" vs. "Pass over" vs. "Pass up"
Choosing between **"Pass by,"** **"Pass over,"** and **"Pass up"** is a common challenge for students in multiple-choice vocabulary tests.

*   **The Rule:** 
    1.  **Pass by** means to physically move past something. Figuratively, when an opportunity "passes you by," it means it slips away without you taking advantage of it. It is mostly intransitive in this figurative sense.
        *   *Example:* I stood at the corner and watched the parade **pass by**.
    2.  **Pass over** is transitive and means to deliberately ignore or bypass someone for a promotion, title, or responsibility.
        *   *Example:* They chose to **pass him over** because of his poor attendance record.
    3.  **Pass up** is transitive and means to willingly decline or reject an offer, opportunity, or invitation.
        *   *Example:* I cannot believe you chose to **pass up** such a lucrative business contract.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Intransitivity Constraints of "Pass away" and "Pass out"
In error-spotting questions, examiners frequently test candidates on the voice of phrasal verbs.

*   **The Rule:** Both **"Pass away"** (to die) and **"Pass out"** (to faint) are strictly **intransitive** phrasal verbs. Since they do not have direct objects, they can never be written in the passive voice. Adding helping verbs like *was/were* or *been* to make them passive is grammatically incorrect.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The old king *was passed away* last year after a brief illness.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The old king **passed away** last year after a brief illness.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The young student *was passed out* during the assembly due to the heat.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The young student **passed out** during the assembly due to the heat.

#### Q1. Spot the Error — Semantic Meaning Conflict (SSC CGL Tier-2 Level)
**Question:** The exhausted marathon runner (I) / staggered across the finish line (II) / and immediately passed up due to extreme fatigue. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (III). The phrasal verb meaning to lose consciousness or faint is "pass out," not "pass up." "Pass up" means to decline or reject an opportunity. Therefore, "passed up" must be replaced with "passed out" to restore the correct meaning of the sentence.
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। बेहोश होने या संज्ञा खोने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब "pass out" (भूतकाल "passed out") होता है, न कि "pass up"। "Pass up" का अर्थ किसी सुनहरे अवसर को अस्वीकार करना या छोड़ना होता है। अतः वाक्य को अर्थपूर्ण और व्याकरणिक रूप से शुद्ध बनाने के लिए "passed up" के स्थान पर "passed out" का प्रयोग किया जाना चाहिए।

**Question:** It was deeply moving to see (I) / the funeral procession pass off, (II) / as thousands of citizens gathered to pay their respects. (III)

(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)

(A) pass off
(B) pass up
(C) pass by
(D) pass over

**Correct Answer - C**

#### Q5. Spot the Error — Intransitive Passive Voice Trap (Bank PO Level)
**Question:** The elderly statesman was passed away peacefully (I) / at his ancestral home last night, (II) / surrounded by his loving family members. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (I). The phrasal verb "pass away" meaning to die is strictly intransitive. Intransitive phrasal verbs cannot be used in passive voice constructions. Therefore, using the auxiliary verb "was" before "passed away" is grammatically incorrect; it should be written in the active past tense as "The elderly statesman passed away peacefully..."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (I) में है। "Pass away" (मृत्यु होना) एक अकर्मक क्रिया (intransitive verb) है। अकर्मक क्रियाओं का पैसिव वॉइस नहीं बनाया जा सकता, इसलिए "passed away" के पहले सहायक क्रिया "was" का प्रयोग व्याकरणिक रूप से पूरी तरह गलत है। इसे सुधारकर एक्टिव वॉइस में केवल **"passed away"** लिखा जाना चाहिए। अतः विकल्प (A) में त्रुटि है।

#### Q10. Comprehensive Syntax Check — Multiple Statement Pattern (SBI PO Mains Level)
**Question:** Identify the grammatically correct sentence(s) from the options provided below:
(I) The graduation parade passed off beautifully under the clear autumn sky.
(II) She fainted and passed out during the grueling drill practice in the afternoon.
(III) The board of trustees passed him over for the managing director's job.

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Both (I) and (III)
(D) All (I), (II), and (III)
(E) None of the sentences are correct

**Correct Answer - D**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** All three sentences are grammatically and syntactically correct. Sentence (I) correctly uses "passed off" to mean took place or happened. Sentence (II) correctly uses "passed out" to mean fainted. Sentence (III) correctly uses "passed him over" to mean bypassed or ignored him for a position.
- **Hindi:** दिए गए तीनों वाक्य व्याकरण और संदर्भ की दृष्टि से पूरी तरह से शुद्ध हैं। वाक्य (I) में परेड के "संपन्न होने" के अर्थ में "passed off" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (II) में भीषण गर्मी में अभ्यास के दौरान "बेहोश होने" के अर्थ में "passed out" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (III) में प्रबंध निदेशक के पद के लिए उम्मीदवार को "नजरअंदाज करने" के अर्थ में "passed him over" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। अतः विकल्प (D) सही उत्तर है।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Pass away" और "Pass out" के बीच क्या मुख्य अंतर है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों के अर्थों में ज़मीन-आसमान का अंतर है:
*   **Pass away:** इसका अर्थ होता है **मृत्यु होना या स्वर्गवास होना** (यह 'die' का एक विनम्र विकल्प है)।
*   **Pass out:** इसका अर्थ होता है **बेहोश होना** (चक्कर आना) या सैन्य/पुलिस अकादमी से कोर्स पूरा करके **दीक्षित होना**।

### Q2. क्या "Pass by" का प्रयोग किसी व्यक्ति को नजरअंदाज करने के लिए किया जा सकता है?
**Answer:** हाँ, आलंकारिक रूप से (figuratively) "Pass by" का प्रयोग किसी अवसर या व्यक्ति के अनदेखा होने के लिए होता है। विशेष रूप से जब हम कहते हैं: *"Don't let the opportunity pass you by"* (अवसर को अपने हाथ से न जाने दें), तब इसका अर्थ बिना लाभ उठाए गुजर जाने से होता है।

### Q3. "The con artist passed over as a doctor" लिखना व्याकरणिक रूप से गलत क्यों है?
**Answer:** यहाँ "डॉक्टर के रूप में खुद को गलत तरीके से पेश करने या ढोंग करने" का संदर्भ है। इस विशिष्ट अर्थ के लिए सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"pass off"** (pass oneself off as) होता है, न कि "pass over"। अतः सही वाक्य **"The con artist passed himself off as a doctor"** होगा।
