## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: The Transitive Split Trap — Pronouns vs. Nouns in Separable Phrasal Verbs
In sentence-correction and error-spotting exercises, candidates often struggle with the correct placement of personal pronouns when dealing with transitive, separable phrasal verbs like **"carry out"** and **"carry off."**

*   **The Rule:** If the direct object of a separable phrasal verb is a personal pronoun (such as *it, him, her, us, them*), the pronoun **must** be placed between the verb and the prepositional particle. Placing the pronoun at the very end is grammatically incorrect. Conversely, if the object is a noun, it can be placed either in the middle or at the very end.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The committee proposed a new set of rules and the director carried out them immediately.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The committee proposed a new set of rules and the director **carried them out** immediately.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The director carried out **the new rules** immediately.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Active vs. Passive Voice with "Carry away" and "Carry through"
In error-spotting questions, examiners frequently swap active and passive voice constructions to test a candidate's understanding of sentence structures.

*   **The Rule:** 
    1.  **Carry away** is primarily used in the passive voice when describing a person's emotional state (e.g., *was carried away*, *got carried away*). Using it in the active voice when describing a person's emotions is grammatically incorrect.
        *   ❌ **Incorrect:** The young audience *carried away* by the spectacular performance.
        *   ✅ **Correct:** The young audience **was carried away** by the spectacular performance.
    2.  **Carry through** is typically used in the active voice when a quality (such as courage, willpower, or stamina) sustains a person through a difficult ordeal.
        *   ❌ **Incorrect:** The team *was carried through* by their determination during the crisis. (Passive, though acceptable, is less common than the active form)
        *   ✅ **Correct:** Their determination **carried them through** the crisis.

#### Q1. Spot the Error — Intransitive Prepositional Deficit (SSC CGL Tier-2 Level)
**Question:** The researchers were instructed (I) / to carry on the clinical trials (II) / despite the initial complaints from the volunteer group. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (II). The phrasal verb "carry on" means "to continue." When it is followed by a direct object representing an activity (in this case, "the clinical trials"), it must be followed by the preposition "with." Simply writing "carry on the clinical trials" is grammatically incomplete. The correct phrase is "carry on with the clinical trials."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (II) में है। "Carry on" का अर्थ जारी रखना होता है। जब इसके बाद किसी सीधे ऑब्जेक्ट (यहाँ "the clinical trials") का प्रयोग किया जाता है, तो इसके बाद "with" प्रीपोजिशन लगाना अनिवार्य होता है। अतः शुद्ध वाक्य **"carry on with the clinical trials"** होना चाहिए। इसलिए विकल्प (B) सही उत्तर है।

#### Q2. Fill in the Blank — Contextual Semantic Distinction (Bank PO Level)
**Question:** Although the military operation was exceptionally dangerous to ________, the sheer discipline of the commandos ________ them through the hostile territory.

**Options:**
(A) carry on / carried over
(B) carry through / carried off
(C) carry out / carried
(D) carry out / carried through
(E) carry forward / carried back

**Correct Answer - D**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The first blank requires a phrasal verb meaning to execute or perform a dangerous operation, which is "carry out." The second blank requires a phrasal verb indicating that the discipline of the commandos sustained or supported them through the hostile territory, which is "carried through." This makes option (D) the only grammatically and logically consistent choice.
- **Hindi:** पहले रिक्त स्थान में सैन्य अभियान (military operation) को "अंजाम देने या पूरा करने" के अर्थ में "carry out" की आवश्यकता है। दूसरे रिक्त स्थान में कमांडो के अनुशासन द्वारा उन्हें दुश्मन के इलाके में "सहारा देने या पार लगाने" के अर्थ में "carried through" की आवश्यकता है। अतः विकल्प (D) सबसे उपयुक्त है।

#### Q3. Phrase Replacement — Separable Object Trap (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** The marketing agency developed a brilliant advertisement, but they failed to **carry out it** before the festival season ended.

**Options:**
(A) carry it out
(B) carry it off
(C) carry on it
(D) carry it through
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The phrasal verb "carry out" is transitive and separable. When its direct object is a personal pronoun like "it," the pronoun must be placed between the verb "carry" and the particle "out." Placing the pronoun at the end ("carry out it") is grammatically incorrect. Therefore, **"carry it out"** is the correct replacement.
- **Hindi:** "Carry out" एक सकर्मक (transitive) और विभाजनीय (separable) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। जब इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई पर्सनल प्रोनाउन (जैसे "it") होता है, तो उसे वर्ब (carry) और पार्टिकल (out) के बीच में रखना अनिवार्य होता है। वाक्य के अंत में प्रोनाउन रखना ("carry out it") व्याकरण की दृष्टि से अशुद्ध है। अतः सही सुधार विकल्प (A) यानी **"carry it out"** होगा।

#### Q5. Spot the Error — Emotional Passive Construction (Bank Clerk Level)
**Question:** The young artist carried away (I) / by the thunderous applause of the audience (II) / and forgot to thank his mentors on stage. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (I). The context describes the artist losing self-control or becoming overly excited due to the applause. In this figurative sense of emotional excitement, "carry away" must be written in the passive voice. Therefore, we must add a helping verb like "was" or "got" before "carried away." The correct sentence should read: "The young artist **was carried away**..."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (I) में है। यहाँ कलाकार द्वारा दर्शकों की तालियों से "अत्यधिक उत्साहित होकर आत्म-नियंत्रण खोने या भावनाओं में बह जाने" का संदर्भ है। इस अर्थ में "carry away" का प्रयोग हमेशा पैसिव वॉइस (passive voice) में होना चाहिए। अतः "carried away" के पहले "was" या "got" सहायक क्रिया का होना आवश्यक है। शुद्ध वाक्य **"The young artist was carried away..."** होगा।

#### Q6. Sentence Improvement — Reliving the Past (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** The slow, nostalgic melody of the flute always **carries me through** to my grandfather's peaceful cottage in the hills.

**Options:**
(A) carries me off
(B) carries me over
(C) carries me back
(D) carries me forward
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The context describes a nostalgic flute melody causing someone to remember their childhood or past experience. The phrasal verb "carry back" means to cause someone to remember a past time. "Carry through" means to sustain during a crisis, which does not fit this emotional and nostalgic context. Thus, **"carries me back"** is the correct improvement.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ बांसुरी की पुरानी धुन द्वारा दादाजी की झोपड़ी और पहाड़ों के "अतीत के दिनों की याद दिलाने" का संदर्भ है। किसी को अतीत की याद दिलाने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"carry back"** होता है। "Carry through" का अर्थ संकट में सहारा देना होता है, जो यहाँ पूरी तरह से असंगत है। अतः सही सुधार विकल्प (C) होगा।

#### Q8. Spot the Error — Active/Passive Pronoun Clash (SSC CGL Tier-1 Level)
**Question:** The difficult journey was carried him through (I) / because of his deep spiritual belief (II) / and the support of his close companions. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (I). The sentence describes his spiritual belief and companion support sustaining him. The phrasal verb "carry through" is transitive and should be used in the active voice here, with the sustaining qualities as the active subject. The correct active structure is: "His deep spiritual belief... **carried him through** the difficult journey." Or if keeping the structure of the sentence, it should be: "He **was carried through** the difficult journey because of..." Using "The difficult journey was carried him through" is grammatically incorrect.
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (I) में है। यहाँ उसकी आध्यात्मिक आस्था द्वारा उसे कठिन यात्रा में "सहारा देने" का संदर्भ है। "carried him through" का प्रयोग यहाँ सक्रिय रूप (active voice) में होना चाहिए था, या फिर पैसिव संरचना में "He was carried through..." होना चाहिए था। "The difficult journey was carried him through" एक गलत और अस्पष्ट संरचना है। अतः विकल्प (A) में त्रुटि है।

#### Q10. Syntax Evaluation - Multiple Selection Pattern (SBI PO Mains Level)
**Question:** Identify the grammatically correct sentence(s) from the options provided below:
(I) The young prodigy managed to carry off the first prize in the national violin competition.
(II) You must carry on with your studies no matter how difficult the circumstances become.
(III) The remaining supplies were carried through by the team to the high-altitude base camp.

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Both (I) and (II)
(D) All (I), (II), and (III)
(E) None of the sentences are correct

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** Sentences (I) and (II) are grammatically correct. Sentence (I) correctly uses "carry off" to mean winning a prize. Sentence (II) correctly uses "carry on with" to mean continuing an activity (studies). Sentence (III) is incorrect because "carry through" means to sustain a person or complete a task; transporting physical goods to a camp is simply represented by the verb "carried" or "transported," making "carried through" semantically incorrect here.
- **Hindi:** वाक्य (I) और (II) पूरी तरह से शुद्ध हैं। वाक्य (I) में पुरस्कार जीतने के अर्थ में "carry off" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (II) में पढ़ाई जारी रखने के अर्थ में "carry on with" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (III) अशुद्ध है क्योंकि भौतिक सामग्री (supplies) को शिविर तक ले जाने के सामान्य अर्थ में "carry through" का प्रयोग करना गलत है। यहाँ केवल "carried" या "transported" का प्रयोग होना चाहिए था। अतः विकल्प (C) सही उत्तर है।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. क्या "Carry on" और "Carry out" को एक-दूसरे के स्थान पर इस्तेमाल किया जा सकता है?
**Answer:** बिल्कुल नहीं। दोनों के अर्थों में ज़मीन-आसमान का अंतर है। "Carry on" का अर्थ होता है किसी काम को **जारी रखना** (continue doing something), जबकि "Carry out" का अर्थ होता है किसी आदेश, कार्य, या योजना को **पूरा करना या क्रियान्वित करना** (execute or perform a task)।

### Q2. परीक्षा में "Carry out" के साथ ऑब्जेक्ट के स्थान को लेकर क्या नियम है?
**Answer:** "Carry out" एक सकर्मक और विभाजनीय (transitive and separable) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। यदि इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई सामान्य नाउन है (जैसे: *the plan*), तो आप इसे अंत में या बीच में रख सकते हैं (*carry out the plan* या *carry the plan out*). लेकिन यदि ऑब्जेक्ट कोई पर्सनल प्रोनाउन है (जैसे: *it, them*), तो उसे हमेशा बीच में ही रखना होगा (*carry it out*).

### Q3. "Carry through" का प्रयोग किसी व्यक्ति के लिए कब किया जाता है?
**Answer:** जब "Carry through" का ऑब्जेक्ट कोई व्यक्ति होता है, तो इसका अर्थ होता है उस व्यक्ति को किसी अत्यंत कठिन परिस्थिति, बीमारी या संकट के समय **मानसिक या शारीरिक सहारा देना** ताकि वह उस संकट से बाहर निकल सके (जैसे: *His faith carried him through the illness*).
