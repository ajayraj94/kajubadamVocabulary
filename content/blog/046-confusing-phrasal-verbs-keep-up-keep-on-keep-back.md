# Confusing Phrasal Verbs: Keep up vs Keep on vs Keep back with Sentences & Hindi Meanings

Phrasal verbs originating from the base verb **"Keep"** are a common source of confusion in competitive exams like **SSC CGL, SBI PO, IBPS PO, and UPSC CDS** . Because these verbs look very similar, candidates often struggle to choose the correct preposition under exam pressure. 

For example, should a business *keep up* its high quality or *keep to* its schedule? Does a speaker *keep on* talking or *keep back* secrets? A minor prepositional shift can transform a sentence from expressing continuous action (*keep on*) to withholding vital facts (*keep back*) or maintaining standards (*keep up*).

To help you build permanent conceptual clarity, the academic editorial team at **kajubadamvocabulary.in** has prepared this deep-dive study guide. Through a bilingual mnemonic story, comparative tables, and highly detailed practice questions, we will unpack these confusing phrasal verbs.

## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: Direct Object Separation Constraints with "Keep down"
The phrasal verb **"keep down"** has two primary meanings: "to suppress/control something" and "to retain food in the stomach." Both of these transitive meanings carry strict separability rules.

*   **The Rule:** "Keep down" is a transitive separable phrasal verb. When the object of the verb is a noun, it can go after the particle "down" or in the middle. However, when the object is a pronoun (like *it*, *them*, *him*, *her*), it **must** split the verb.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The protest was growing loud, but the police managed to keep down *it* quickly.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The protest was growing loud, but the police managed to **keep it down** quickly.
*   *Explanation:* Since the direct object is the pronoun "it" (referring to the protest), it must be placed between "keep" and "down."

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Semantic Boundaries: "Keep on" vs. "Go on" vs. "Carry on"
Candidates often use these three continuation phrasal verbs interchangeably, which can lead to errors in precise cloze tests.

1.  **Keep on (Persistence/Repetition):** Highlights a persistent, repetitive, and sometimes annoying action.
    *   *Example:* He **keeps on** interrupting me while I am trying to explain the project. (Correct)
    *   *Note:* Strictly followed by a gerund (V-ing form).
2.  **Go on (Progression to a New Stage):** Focuses on progressing or continuing to do something, often moving to a different activity.
    *   *Example:* After completing his bachelor's degree, he **went on to study** law at Harvard. (Correct)
    *   *Note:* Can be followed by an infinitive to show a change of activity.
3.  **Carry on (Maintenance despite Difficulty):** Focuses on continuing a task or life in general, especially despite facing obstacles.
    *   *Example:* Despite the heavy rain, the workers decided to **carry on** with the construction. (Correct)

#### Q1. Fill in the Blank — Maintaining High Quality
**Question:** The chief quality inspector emphasized that the factory must ________ its rigorous testing standards to retain its international certification.

**Options:**
(A) keep down
(B) keep up
(C) keep off
(D) keep back
(E) keep from

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The sentence discusses the factory needing to maintain its rigorous testing standards. The phrasal verb "keep up" means to maintain or sustain a high standard, quality, or level of performance, which fits perfectly. "Keep down" means to suppress, which is incorrect in this context.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ कारखाने द्वारा अपने अंतरराष्ट्रीय प्रमाणीकरण (international certification) को बनाए रखने के लिए कठोर परीक्षण मानकों को "बनाए रखने" का संदर्भ है। किसी स्तर, गुणवत्ता या मानक को बनाए रखने के लिए सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"keep up"** होता है। "Keep down" का अर्थ दबाना या नियंत्रित करना होता है, जो यहाँ असंगत है।

#### Q2. Spot the Error — Gerund Constraint Violation
**Question:** The persistent project manager (I) / decided to keep on to debug (II) / the software code until midnight. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (II). The phrasal verb "keep on" (meaning to continue doing something) is a prepositional phrase that must be followed directly by a gerund (V-ing form). Using the infinitive structure "to debug" is grammatically incorrect. It must be changed to **"keep on debugging."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (II) में है। "Keep on" (लगातार जारी रखना) के बाद हमेशा जेरंड (V-ing form) का प्रयोग किया जाना चाहिए। यहाँ इन्फिनिटिव संरचना "to debug" का प्रयोग अशुद्ध है, इसके स्थान पर जेरंड रूप **"keep on debugging"** होना चाहिए।

#### Q6. Fill in the Blank — Restraint Preposition Match
**Question:** The massive brick wall was tall enough to keep the wild predators from ________ the agricultural fields.

**Options:**
(A) enter
(B) to enter
(C) entering
(D) entered
(E) enters

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The phrase "keep [someone/something] from" means to prevent or stop them from doing an action, and it must be followed strictly by a gerund (V-ing form). Therefore, "entering" is the correct choice. The base form "enter" or infinitive "to enter" are grammatically incorrect.
- **Hindi:** "keep from" (रोकना या बचाना) के बाद हमेशा जेरंड (V-ing form) का प्रयोग किया जाता है। जंगली जानवरों को कृषि क्षेत्रों में प्रवेश करने से "रोकने" के संदर्भ में "from" के बाद जेरंड रूप **"entering"** का प्रयोग ही व्याकरण की दृष्टि से शुद्ध है। अतः विकल्प (C) सही उत्तर है।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Keep up" और "Keep on" के बीच मुख्य व्यावहारिक अंतर क्या है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों के बीच का अंतर उनके अर्थ में निहित है:
*   **Keep up:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी स्तर, गुणवत्ता, गति या मानक को लगातार बनाए रखना ताकि वह नीचे न गिरे (जैसे: *Keep up the good work*).
*   **Keep on:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी क्रिया को लगातार जारी रखना या बार-बार करना (जैसे: *He kept on asking questions*). इसके बाद हमेशा जेरंड (V-ing) आता है.

### Q2. "Keep to" का प्रयोग "Keep off" से किस प्रकार भिन्न है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों में मुख्य अंतर इस प्रकार है :
*   **Keep to:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी निर्धारित मार्ग, नियम, योजना या सीमा का कड़ाई से पालन करना (जैसे: *Keep to the left* या *Keep to the rules*).
*   **Keep off:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी भौतिक सतह (जैसे नव-निर्मित सड़क, घास, या गीला फर्श) से दूर रहना या उस पर कदम न रखना (जैसे: *Keep off the grass*) .

### Q3. क्या "Keep back" का प्रयोग केवल अमूर्त (Abstract) चीज़ों के लिए होता है?
**Answer:** नहीं, "Keep back" का प्रयोग भौतिक और अमूर्त दोनों संदर्भों में होता है:
1. **अमूर्त (Abstract):** किसी तथ्य या जानकारी को छिपाना या साझा न करना (जैसे: *She decided to keep back the truth*).
2. **भौतिक (Physical):** किसी खतरनाक स्थान या आग से शारीरिक रूप से सुरक्षित दूरी बनाए रखना (जैसे: *Keep back from the yellow line*).
