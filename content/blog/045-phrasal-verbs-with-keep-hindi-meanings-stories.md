# Phrasal Verbs with 'Keep' with Hindi Meanings, Stories & Memory Tricks

In competitive examinations like **SSC CGL Tier 2, SBI PO, IBPS PO, and UPSC CDS**, phrasal verbs are a frequent source of tricky questions. Among these, the verb family constructed around **"Keep"** is highly targeted by examiners. Phrasal verbs like *keep up*, *keep off*, *keep on*, and *keep back* may look simple, but their contextual meanings and grammar rules can be highly deceptive [1]. 

A slight change in the preposition can alter a sentence's meaning entirely—transforming it from maintaining a fast pace (*keep up with*) to avoiding stepping on a surface (*keep off*) or concealing vital facts (*keep back*). ## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: The Gerund Constraint of "Keep on" and "Keep from"
One of the most frequent grammar traps tested in error spotting is the grammatical structure following **"keep on"** and **"keep from."**

*   **The Rule:** Both "keep on" (meaning to continue) and "keep from" (meaning to restrain/prevent) function as prepositional phrasal verbs. Consequently, any verb that follows them directly **must** be in the gerund form (the V-ing form functioning as a noun). Using the base form of the verb (V1) or an infinitive is a severe grammatical error.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The dedicated student decided to keep on *study* even after the library closed.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The dedicated student decided to **keep on studying** even after the library closed.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** We could not keep the rain from *destroy* our newly harvested crops.
*   ✅ **Correct:** We could not keep the rain from **destroying** our newly harvested crops.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Separability Dynamics of "Keep back" (Information Concealment)
Understanding where to place direct objects and pronouns with **"keep back"** is critical for sentence improvement.

*   **The Rule:** When "keep back" is used transitively to mean "withholding or concealing information," it behaves as a separable phrasal verb. If the object is a noun phrase (e.g., the truth, the facts), it can be placed at the end or in the middle. However, if the object is replaced by a pronoun (such as *it*, *them*), the pronoun **must** split the verb.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The witness knew the truth about the accident but decided to keep back *it* from the investigators.
*   ✅ **Correct:** The witness knew the truth about the accident but decided to **keep it back** from the investigators.

#### Q1. Fill in the Blank — Adhering to Limits
**Question:** To maintain the strict confidentiality of the research, the scientists were advised to ________ the approved guidelines and avoid discussing the project with outsiders.

**Options:**
(A) keep back
(B) keep out
(C) keep to
(D) keep off
(E) keep on

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The sentence describes scientists being advised to adhere strictly to the approved guidelines. The phrasal verb "keep to" means to adhere strictly to a path, plan, rule, or limit, which fits this context. "Keep off" means to stay away from a physical area, which is incorrect here.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ वैज्ञानिकों को अनुसंधान की गोपनीयता बनाए रखने के लिए स्वीकृत दिशानिर्देशों (approved guidelines) का "दृढ़ता से पालन करने" की सलाह दी जा रही है। किसी नियम, योजना या सीमा का पालन करने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"keep to"** होता है। "Keep off" का अर्थ भौतिक स्थान से दूर रहना होता है, जो यहाँ असंगत है।

#### Q2. Spot the Error — Missing Preposition with Comparative Speed (Bank PO Level)
**Question:** Because technology is evolving at an exponential rate, (I) / traditional companies are struggling (II) / to keep up their tech-savvy competitors. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). The context describes traditional companies struggling to maintain the same pace as their competitors. The correct phrasal verb for moving or progressing at the same speed as someone or something is "keep up with." Dropping the preposition "with" is incorrect; the sentence must end with "to **keep up with** their tech-savvy competitors."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। यहाँ पारंपरिक कंपनियों के अपने प्रतिस्पर्धियों की "समान गति से आगे बढ़ने या उनके साथ कदम मिलाकर चलने" में संघर्ष करने का संदर्भ है। किसी के बराबर गति से चलने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"keep up with"** होता है। केवल "keep up" लिखना अशुद्ध है, इसके साथ "with" का प्रयोग अनिवार्य है।

#### Q3. Sentence Improvement — Gerund Constraint of Continuation
**Question:** Despite facing severe criticism from the media, the politician decided to **keep on to defend** his controversial statement.

**Options:**
(A) keep on defending
(B) keep up defending
(C) keep off to defend
(D) keep back defending
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The politician decided to continue defending his statement. As explained in Grammar Spotlight 1, the phrasal verb "keep on" must be followed directly by a gerund (V-ing form). Using the infinitive "to defend" after "keep on" is incorrect. Therefore, "keep on defending" is the correct improvement.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ राजनेता द्वारा आलोचना के बावजूद अपने बयान का बचाव "जारी रखने" का संदर्भ है। नियम के अनुसार, "keep on" के बाद सीधे जेरंड (V-ing form) का प्रयोग किया जाना चाहिए। इन्फिनिटिव "to defend" का प्रयोग यहाँ अशुद्ध है, अतः सही सुधार **"keep on defending"** होगा।

#### Q5. Spot the Error — Pronoun Separation Constraint with "Keep back"
**Question:** The primary suspect confessed to the theft (I) / but the police suspected that (II) / he was still trying to keep back it from the chief investigator. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). "Keep back" (meaning to withhold or conceal information) is a transitive separable phrasal verb. When the direct object is a pronoun (here, "it" referring to the information), it must be placed in the middle. Therefore, "keep back it" must be corrected to **"keep it back."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। जानकारी "छिपाने या दबाने" के अर्थ में "keep back" एक separable (विभाजित होने वाला) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। जब इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई सर्वनाम होता है (यहाँ सूचना के लिए "it"), तो उस सर्वनाम को वर्ब और पार्टिकल के बीच में रखा जाना चाहिए। अतः "keep back it" के स्थान पर **"keep it back"** होना चाहिए।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Keep off" और "Keep out" के प्रयोग में क्या मुख्य अंतर है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों के बीच मुख्य व्यावहारिक अंतर इस प्रकार है:
1. **Keep off:** इसका प्रयोग मुख्य रूप से किसी सतह (जैसे घास, गीला फर्श, या रेलवे ट्रैक) पर कदम रखने या उसके संपर्क में आने से बचने के लिए किया जाता है (जैसे: *Keep off the grass*).
2. **Keep out:** इसका प्रयोग किसी त्रिविमीय स्थान (3D space) जैसे कमरा, इमारत, बाड़ से घिरे क्षेत्र या खतरनाक क्षेत्र में प्रवेश करने से रोकने के लिए किया जाता है (जैसे: *Danger! Keep out*).

### Q2. "Keep up" और "Keep up with" में क्या अंतर है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों में मुख्य अंतर इस प्रकार है [1]:
*   **Keep up:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी स्तर, गुणवत्ता या गति को बिना गिरे लगातार बनाए रखना (जैसे: *Keep up the good work*).
*   **Keep up with:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी अन्य व्यक्ति या बदलती हुई स्थिति (जैसे तकनीक या फैशन) के साथ कदम मिलाकर चलना ताकि आप उनसे पीछे न छूटें (जैसे: *It is hard to keep up with the latest fashion*).

### Q3. "Keep back" का अर्थ क्या केवल जानकारी छिपाना ही होता है?
**Answer:** नहीं, "Keep back" के दो अलग-अलग अर्थ होते हैं:
1. **शारीरिक रूप से पीछे रहना (Physical Distance):** किसी खतरनाक क्षेत्र या आग से सुरक्षित दूरी बनाए रखना (जैसे: *The police told the crowd to keep back*).
2. **जानकारी छिपाना (Conceal Information):** किसी तथ्य या सच्चाई को दूसरों से छिपाना या प्रकट न करना (जैसे: *Do not keep back any details of the deal*).
