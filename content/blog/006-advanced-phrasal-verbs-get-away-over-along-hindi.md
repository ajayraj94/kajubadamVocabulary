# Advanced 'Get' Phrasal Verbs (Get away, Get over, Get along) Explained in Hindi

In advanced competitive examinations like **SSC CGL Tier 2**, **IBPS PO**, and **SBI PO Mains**, examiners frequently move beyond basic vocabulary to test multi-word verbs and complex prepositional combinations. Among these, the advanced variants of the verb **"Get"**—such as *get away*, *get over*, and *get along*—are highly favored. 

These phrasal verbs often carry multiple meanings depending on the context. For example, while "get away" can mean physically escaping a pursuer, in corporate contexts it often means taking a brief vacation to relax. ## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: The Three-word Phrasal Verb Structure — "Get round to"
One of the most frequent error-spotting traps in banking and SSC exams involves three-word phrasal verbs like **"get round to"** (often written as *get around to* in American English).

*   **The Rule:** In three-word phrasal verbs, the final word is a **preposition**, not an infinitive marker. Therefore, any verb that follows "get round to" must be a **gerund (V-ing form)** or a noun, rather than a base verb.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** After months of delay, the management finally got round to *review* the performance of the sales team.
*   "review" is a base verb, which violates the prepositional constraint of "to".
*   ✅ **Correct:** After months of delay, the management finally got round to **reviewing** the performance of the sales team.
*   "reviewing" is a gerund, acting correctly as the object of the preposition.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Semantic Divergence in "Get on with"
Candidates often get confused because **"get on with"** exhibits two completely different meanings depending on the context of the sentence:

1.  **To Continue a Task (Progress):**
    *   *Example:* Please stop gossiping and **get on with** your assignments. (यहाँ इसका अर्थ काम जारी रखने से है).
2.  **To Have a Friendly Relationship (Harmony):**
    *   *Example:* I find it very easy to **get on with** my new roommates. (यहाँ इसका अर्थ दोस्ताना संबंध होने से है—जो कि *get along with* का समानार्थी है).
*   **Exam Strategy:** When solving cloze tests or phrase replacement questions, identify whether the sentence discusses "work/progress" or "people/relationships" to choose the correct synonym.

#### Q1. Spot the Error — Three-word Phrasal Gerund Trap
**Question:** Although the administrative head promised (I) / to address our grievances, he has not (II) / got round to analyze the official complaint files yet. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (III) of the sentence. The phrasal verb "got round to" ends with the preposition "to," which requires a gerund (V-ing form) as its object. Therefore, the base verb "analyze" must be replaced with the gerund "analyzing." This is a high-frequency exam pattern.
- **Hindi:** वाक्य के भाग (III) में त्रुटि है। फ्रेज़ल वर्ब "got round to" का अंत "to" preposition से होता है, जिसके बाद ऑब्जेक्ट के रूप में हमेशा gerund (V-ing रूप) आना चाहिए। इसलिए, क्रिया के मूल रूप "analyze" के स्थान पर "analyzing" का प्रयोग किया जाना चाहिए।

#### Q4. Spot the Error — Inseparability Rules
**Question:** It is never easy for a dedicated player (I) / to get his team's unexpected defeat over (II) / and start preparing for the next tournament. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Both (I) and (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (II). The phrasal verb "get over" (meaning to recover from a shock or defeat) is strictly inseparable. This means that its object ("his team's unexpected defeat") cannot be placed between the verb and the preposition. The correct construction must be "to get over his team's unexpected defeat."
- **Hindi:** वाक्य के भाग (II) में त्रुटि है। फ्रेज़ल वर्ब "get over" (किसी सदमे या हार से उबरना) एक inseparable (विभाजित न होने वाला) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। इसका अर्थ यह है कि इसका ऑब्जेक्ट ("his team's unexpected defeat") कभी भी वर्ब और प्रीपोजीशन के बीच में नहीं आ सकता। सही वाक्य "to get over his team's unexpected defeat" होना चाहिए।

#### Q7. Spot the Error — Evading Responsibility
**Question:** The young employee tried to get out (I) / from attending the mandatory training session (II) / by producing a falsified medical certificate. (III)

(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Both (I) and (II)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**

The sentence incorrectly splits this into "get out" in part (I) and "from" in part (II). The correct phrase should be "to get out of attending..."

#### Q10. Spot the Error — Passive Voice Transformation
**Question:** The new director is highly cooperative, (I) / making it very easy for him (II) / to be gotten along with by his staff members. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** Part (III) is highly awkward and grammatically incorrect. Phrasal verbs like "get along with" are intransitive in nature when representing a state of relationship, and they do not easily undergo passive voice transformations. The sentence should be written in active voice: "making it very easy for his staff members to get along with him."
- **Hindi:** वाक्य का भाग (III) अत्यंत अजीब और व्याकरण की दृष्टि से अशुद्ध है। "Get along with" जैसे फ्रेज़ल वर्ब जो संबंध या आपसी तालमेल की स्थिति को दर्शाते हैं, वे स्वभाव से अकर्मक (intransitive) होते हैं, और उनका पैसिव वॉइस (passive voice) रूपांतरण नहीं किया जाना चाहिए। वाक्य को एक्टिव वॉइस में "making it very easy for his staff members to get along with him" लिखा जाना चाहिए।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Get along with" और "Get on with" के प्रयोग में क्या अंतर है?
**Answer:** "Get along with" का प्रयोग केवल लोगों के बीच दोस्ताना संबंध और आपसी तालमेल दर्शाने के लिए किया जाता है (जैसे: *I get along with my boss*). वहीं "Get on with" के दो अर्थ होते हैं: पहला, किसी कार्य को जारी रखना (जैसे: *get on with your work*), और दूसरा, लोगों के साथ दोस्ताना संबंध होना (इस अर्थ में यह *get along with* का ही समानार्थी है)।

### Q2. "Get round to" के बाद क्रिया में 'ing' क्यों लगाया जाता है?
**Answer:** "Get round to" (या get around to) में आने वाला शब्द **"to" एक Preposition है**, न कि Infinitive marker। अंग्रेजी व्याकरण के नियम के अनुसार, किसी भी Preposition के तुरंत बाद आने वाली क्रिया हमेशा **Gerund (V-ing form)** के रूप में होनी चाहिए। इसलिए हमेशा *get round to doing* लिखा जाता है, न कि *get round to do*।

### Q3. "Get away" और "Get away with" में क्या अंतर है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों में काफी बड़ा अंतर है:
1. **Get away:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी स्थान से शारीरिक रूप से दूर जाना या छुट्टी मनाने के लिए कहीं बाहर जाना (जैसे: *I need to get away for a few days*).
2. **Get away with:** इसका अर्थ होता है कोई गलत काम या अपराध करके भी बिना किसी सजा या दंड के बच निकलना (जैसे: *The thief got away with the crime*).
