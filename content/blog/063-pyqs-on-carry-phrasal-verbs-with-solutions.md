# Previous Year Questions (PYQs) on 'Carry' Phrasal Verbs with Solutions

In competitive examinations like **SSC CGL, SBI PO, IBPS PO, UPSC CDS, and NDA**, vocabulary questions testing phrasal verbs are highly common. Among these, phrasal verbs derived from the base verb **"Carry"** are frequently used by examiners to construct tricky questions. Because verbs like *carry out*, *carry on*, and *carry through* appear visually similar, candidates often choose the incorrect option under exam pressure.

To help you perform confidently, the academic editorial team at **kajubadamvocabulary.in** has compiled actual and simulated previous year questions (PYQs). ## 📝 The Core Vocabulary Masterclass — मुख्य शब्दकोश तालिका

This table breaks down the syntax, meanings, and common exam traps of the eight key phrasal verbs with **"Carry"** tested in recent exams.

| Phrasal Verb | English Meaning | Hindi Meaning | Exam-Style Example | Grammar Trap / Key Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Carry out** | To execute, perform, or conduct a task, order, or experiment. | आदेश का पालन करना, कार्य पूरा करना या प्रयोग करना। | The laboratory technician must **carry out** the safety inspection every morning. | Transitive; requires a direct object representing a task, duty, or order. |
| **Carry on** | To continue doing something, or to behave in an excited/anxious way. | काम जारी रखना; या उत्तेजित होकर व्यवहार करना। | Despite the heavy rainfall, the construction workers decided to **carry on**. | Often followed by \"with\" plus a noun/gerund when transitive. |
| **Carry through** | To sustain someone or complete a task successfully despite immense difficulties. | कठिन समय में सहारा देना या किसी कठिन कार्य को अंत तक पूरा करना। | Your courage during this personal crisis will **carry you through**. | Can take a person as an object (meaning to sustain them through a crisis). |
| **Carry away** | To lose self-control due to strong emotion or excitement. | भावनाओं या उत्साह में बह जाना/आप खो देना। | Do not get **carried away** by early stock market gains; invest cautiously. | Usually used in passive voice constructions: \"be/get carried away.\" |
| **Carry over** | To transfer, extend, or apply to a different time, place, or situation. | एक परिस्थिति से दूसरी परिस्थिति में स्थानांतरित या लागू होना। | Any unused leaves from this month will **carry over** to the next month. | Often intransitive when describing elements persisting into another phase. |
| **Carry off** | To win a prize, or to handle a difficult situation successfully. | पुरस्कार जीतना या किसी कठिन परिस्थिति को सफलतापूर्वक संभाल लेना। | She was incredibly nervous, but she managed to **carry off** the presentation. | Distinct from \"carry out.\" \"Carry off\" emphasizes flair or successful completion of a difficult role. |
| **Carry forward** | To transfer a cumulative total, an idea, or a legacy to a future stage or page. | अगले पृष्ठ या चरण पर ले जाना; आगे बढ़ाना। | The accounting department needs to **carry forward** the net profit to the new ledger. | Commonly used in accounting/financial contexts and legacy-building. |
| **Carry back** | To cause someone to remember a past time or experience. | अतीत की याद दिलाना। | Listening to the old cassette tapes always **carries me back** to the nineties. | Separable phrasal verb; requires a personal pronoun/noun in the middle. |

## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: Prepositional Collisions — "Carry on with" vs. "Carry out"
In advanced sentence improvement sections, candidates are often tested on the syntax of **"Carry on"** when it takes an object compared to **"Carry out."**

*   **The Rule:** 
    1.  **Carry out** is a direct transitive phrasal verb. It takes an object directly without requiring any middle preposition.
        *   *Example:* The soldiers **carried out** the commander's orders. (Correct)
    2.  **Carry on** is primarily intransitive, meaning "to continue." However, when it is used transitivity to mean "to continue with an activity," it **must** be paired with the preposition **"with"** followed by a noun or gerund. Dropping "with" in such transitive contexts is a common grammatical error.
        *   ❌ **Incorrect:** We need to *carry on our work* despite the noise.
        *   ✅ **Correct:** We need to **carry on with** our work despite the noise.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Active vs. Passive Voice Dynamics with "Carry away"
In error-spotting questions, examiners frequently exploit the differences between active and passive constructions of **"Carry away."**

*   **The Rule:** The phrasal verb **"Carry away"** in its physical sense means "to take away by force or water." However, in figurative, exam-tested contexts, it means "to lose judgment or self-control due to excitement." In this figurative sense, the subject is almost always the recipient of the emotion, requiring a **passive construction** (using *was/were/got carried away*). Using it in the active voice when describing a person's emotional state is grammatically incorrect.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** During the concert, the young fans *carried away* by the energetic performance of the lead singer.
*   ✅ **Correct:** During the concert, the young fans **were carried away** by the energetic performance of the lead singer.

#### Q1. Spot the Error — Voice and Meaning Conflict (SSC CGL Tier-2 Level)
**Question:** The enthusiastic crowd (I) / carried away by the politician's promise (II) / of immediate tax reforms and free housing. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (II). The context describes the crowd losing self-control or becoming overly excited by the politician's promise. In this figurative sense, "carry away" must be written in the passive voice. Therefore, we must add the helping verb "was" or "were" (since "crowd" can take a plural or singular verb depending on context; here, "was/were carried away" is needed). The correct construction is "were carried away by..."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (II) में है। यहाँ भीड़ द्वारा नेता के वादों से "अत्यधिक उत्साहित होकर आत्म-नियंत्रण खोने" का संदर्भ है। इस अर्थ में "carry away" का प्रयोग हमेशा पैसिव वॉइस (passive voice) में होना चाहिए। अतः "carried away" के पहले सहायक क्रिया "was" या "were" का होना आवश्यक है। इसे सुधारकर **"were carried away by"** लिखा जाना चाहिए।

#### Q2. Fill in the Blank — Contextual Semantic Matching (Bank PO Level)
**Question:** Though the operational tasks were exceptionally difficult to ________, the project leader's calm demeanor ________ the entire team through the worst phase of the project.

**Options:**
(A) carry on / carried over
(B) carry out / carried
(C) carry out / carried through
(D) carry off / carried on
(E) carry forward / carried back

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The first blank requires a phrasal verb meaning to execute or perform operational tasks, which is "carry out." The second blank requires a phrasal verb indicating that the leader's calm demeanor sustained or supported the team through a difficult phase, which is "carried through." This makes option (C) the only grammatically and logically consistent choice.
- **Hindi:** पहले रिक्त स्थान में परिचालन कार्यों (operational tasks) को "क्रियान्वित करने या पूरा करने" के अर्थ में "carry out" की आवश्यकता है। दूसरे रिक्त स्थान में टीम को कठिन चरण में "सहारा देने या पार लगाने" के अर्थ में "carried through" की आवश्यकता है। अतः विकल्प (C) सबसे उपयुक्त है।

#### Q3. Phrase Replacement — Separable Pronoun Rule (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** The marketing department proposed a radical campaign, and the executive board requested them to **carry out it** immediately.

**Options:**
(A) carry it out
(B) carry it off
(C) carry on it
(D) carry it through
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The phrasal verb "carry out" is transitive and separable. When its direct object is a personal pronoun like "it," the pronoun must be placed between the verb "carry" and the particle "out." Placing the pronoun at the end ("carry out it") is grammatically incorrect. Therefore, **"carry it out"** is the correct replacement.
- **Hindi:** "Carry out" एक सकर्मक (transitive) और विभाजनीय (separable) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। जब इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई पर्सनल प्रोनाउन (जैसे "it") होता है, तो उसे वर्ब (carry) और पार्टिकल (out) के बीच में रखना अनिवार्य होता है। वाक्य के अंत में प्रोनाउन रखना ("carry out it") व्याकरण की दृष्टि से अशुद्ध है। अतः सही सुधार विकल्प (A) यानी **"carry it out"** होगा।

#### Q5. Spot the Error — Intransitive vs. Transitive Preposition (Bank Clerk Level)
**Question:** The local administration decided (I) / to carry on the relief work (II) / despite the severe logistical challenges in the flooded areas. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (I) and (II)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (II). When the phrasal verb "carry on" is used transitively with an object (in this case, "the relief work") to mean continuing an activity, it must be followed by the preposition "with." Simply writing "carry on the relief work" is grammatically incomplete. It should be corrected to **"carry on with the relief work."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (II) में है। जब "carry on" का प्रयोग किसी ऑब्जेक्ट (यहाँ "the relief work") के साथ काम को जारी रखने के अर्थ में किया जाता है, तो इसके बाद "with" प्रीपोजिशन का प्रयोग अनिवार्य रूप से होना चाहिए। अतः शुद्ध वाक्य **"carry on with the relief work"** होगा। इसलिए विकल्प (B) में त्रुटि है।

#### Q6. Sentence Improvement — Nostalgia/Memory Context (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** The aromatic scent of the wet soil always **carries me through** to my childhood days on the countryside farm.

**Options:**
(A) carries me off
(B) carries me over
(C) carries me back
(D) carries me forward
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The context describes a sensory experience (the scent of wet soil) causing someone to remember their childhood. The phrasal verb "carry back" means to cause someone to remember a past time. "Carry through" means to sustain during a crisis, which is incorrect in this nostalgic context. Thus, **"carries me back"** is the correct improvement.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ मिट्टी की सोंधी खुशबू द्वारा बचपन के दिनों की "याद दिलाने" का संदर्भ है। किसी को अतीत की याद दिलाने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"carry back"** होता है। "Carry through" का अर्थ संकट में सहारा देना होता है, जो यहाँ पूरी तरह से असंगत है। अतः सही सुधार विकल्प (C) होगा।

#### Q8. Spot the Error — Conflict of Tense and Meaning (SSC CGL Tier-1 Level)
**Question:** His immense physical stamina (I) / and mental resilience (II) / was carried him off during the grueling marathon. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). The sentence has two errors: first, the auxiliary verb "was" makes the construction passive, but the subject ("stamina and resilience") actively sustained him, so it should be in the active voice. Second, the phrasal verb used is "carried him off" (which means won or died/removed), but the context requires "carried him through" (sustained him during a difficult ordeal). Thus, part (III) should read **"carried him through during the grueling marathon."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। यहाँ दो बड़ी त्रुटियाँ हैं: पहली, "was" का प्रयोग करके वाक्य को पैसिव बनाया गया है जबकि वाक्य एक्टिव वॉइस (active voice) में होना चाहिए। दूसरी, "carried him off" का प्रयोग किया गया है, जबकि मैराथन जैसी कठिन परिस्थिति में "सहारा देने या पार लगाने" के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"carried him through"** होना चाहिए। अतः विकल्प (C) में त्रुटि है।

#### Q10. Comprehensive Syntax Check — Multiple Choice Pattern (SBI PO Mains Level)
**Question:** Identify the grammatically correct sentence(s) from the options provided below:
(I) The young prodigy managed to carry off the first prize in the national violin competition.
(II) You must carry on with your studies no matter how difficult the circumstances become.
(III) The remaining supplies were carried through by the team to the high-altitude base camp.

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Both (I) and (II)
(D) All (I), (II), and (III)
(E) None of the sentences are correct

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** Sentences (I) and (II) are grammatically correct. Sentence (I) correctly uses "carry off" to mean winning a prize. Sentence (II) correctly uses "carry on with" to mean continuing an activity (studies). Sentence (III) is incorrect because "carry through" means to sustain a person or complete a task; transporting physical goods to a camp is simply represented by the verb "carried" or "transported," making "carried through" semantically incorrect here.
- **Hindi:** वाक्य (I) और (II) पूरी तरह से शुद्ध हैं। वाक्य (I) में पुरस्कार जीतने के अर्थ में "carry off" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (II) में पढ़ाई जारी रखने के अर्थ में "carry on with" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (III) अशुद्ध है क्योंकि भौतिक सामग्री (supplies) को शिविर तक ले जाने के सामान्य अर्थ में "carry through" का प्रयोग करना गलत है। यहाँ केवल "carried" या "transported" का प्रयोग होना चाहिए था। अतः विकल्प (C) सही उत्तर है।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. क्या "Carry on" और "Carry out" को एक-दूसरे के स्थान पर इस्तेमाल किया जा सकता है?
**Answer:** बिल्कुल नहीं। दोनों के अर्थों में ज़मीन-आसमान का अंतर है। "Carry on" का अर्थ होता है किसी काम को **जारी रखना** (continue doing something), जबकि "Carry out" का अर्थ होता है किसी आदेश, कार्य, या योजना को **पूरा करना या क्रियान्वित करना** (execute or perform a task)।

### Q2. परीक्षा में "Carry out" के साथ ऑब्जेक्ट के स्थान को लेकर क्या नियम है?
**Answer:** "Carry out" एक सकर्मक और विभाजनीय (transitive and separable) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। यदि इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई सामान्य नाउन है (जैसे: *the plan*), तो आप इसे अंत में या बीच में रख सकते हैं (*carry out the plan* या *carry the plan out*). लेकिन यदि ऑब्जेक्ट कोई पर्सनल प्रोनाउन है (जैसे: *it, them*), तो उसे हमेशा बीच में ही रखना होगा (*carry it out*).

### Q3. "Carry through" का प्रयोग किसी व्यक्ति के लिए कब किया जाता है?
**Answer:** जब "Carry through" का ऑब्जेक्ट कोई व्यक्ति होता है, तो इसका अर्थ होता है उस व्यक्ति को किसी अत्यंत कठिन परिस्थिति, बीमारी या संकट के समय **मानसिक या शारीरिक सहारा देना** ताकि वह उस संकट से बाहर निकल सके (जैसे: *His faith carried him through the illness*).
