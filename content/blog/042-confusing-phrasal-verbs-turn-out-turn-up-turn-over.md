# Confusing Phrasal Verbs: Turn out vs Turn up vs Turn over with Sentences & Hindi Meanings

Among the vast array of phrasal verbs in the English language, those originating from the base verb **"Turn"** present unique challenges [1]. In particular, **"Turn out," "Turn up,"** and **"Turn over"** are highly confusing because each one carries multiple, distinct meanings depending on the context [1]. A student preparing for competitive examinations like **SSC CGL, SBI PO, IBPS PO, or UPSC CDS** cannot afford to guess their meanings blindly, as examiners frequently use these subtle shifts to design tricky close tests and error-detection questions.

For instance, does a lost object *turn out* or *turn up*? Does a business *turn up* a profit or *turn over* millions? To build permanent semantic clarity, this guide by the academic team at **kajubadamvocabulary.in** uses a contextual narrative, followed by a comparative masterclass and ten advanced mock questions to help you achieve absolute precision in your examinations.

## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: The Phrasal Verb "Turn over" vs. The Noun "Turnover"
Candidates frequently confuse the two-word phrasal verb **"turn over"** with the single-word noun **"turnover."**

1.  **Turn over (Phrasal Verb):** This is a two-word action verb. When expressing the act of generating sales or transferring assets, it requires standard verb inflections (such as *turned over*, *turning over*).
    *   *Example:* The retail giant hopes to **turn over** millions in the third quarter. (Correct)
2.  **Turnover (Noun):** This is a single, closed-compound noun that refers to either the total financial revenue generated or the rate at which employees leave an organization. It cannot function as an action verb.
    *   *Example:* The company experienced a high employee **turnover** last year. (Correct)
    *   ❌ *Incorrect:* The business managed to turnover millions of dollars this year. (Using the noun form as a verb is a common error).

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Collective Intransitivity of "Turn up" vs. "Turn out" in Crowd Contexts
In error correction and fill-in-the-blank questions, candidates are often tested on whether to use **"turn up"** or **"turn out"** when describing groups of people.

*   **The Key Distinction:**
    *   Use **"Turn up"** when referring to the arrival or appearance of *specific individuals*, especially when they are late or unexpected.
        *   *Example:* Only three members of the committee **turned up** for the urgent meeting. (Correct)
    *   Use **"Turn out"** when referring to a *mass crowd, public gathering, or collective assembly* of citizens for a specific event, vote, or protest.
        *   *Example:* Thousands of citizens **turned out** to cast their votes in the general election. (Correct)
        *   ❌ *Incorrect:* Thousands of citizens turned up to cast their votes in the general election. (While understandable, "turned out" is the standard, high-yield idiomatic choice for mass public mobilization).

#### Q1. Fill in the Blank — Locating Lost Objects
**Question:** The security agency was relieved when the critical encryption keys, which had been missing for three days, finally ________ during a routine desk inspection.

**Options:**
(A) turned out
(B) turned up
(C) turned over
(D) turned in
(E) turned to

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The context describes lost or missing keys suddenly being found or discovered. The phrasal verb "turn up" means to be found or discovered after being lost, which fits this context. "Turn out" means to prove to be, and "turn over" means to hand over control, neither of which makes sense here.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ संदर्भ तीन दिनों से लापता महत्वपूर्ण एन्क्रिप्शन कुंजियों के अचानक मिल जाने से है। खोई हुई वस्तु के अचानक मिल जाने या सामने आने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"turn up"** होता है। "Turn out" का अर्थ साबित होना और "Turn over" का अर्थ सौंपना होता है, इसलिए विकल्प (B) सही है।

#### Q2. Spot the Error — Noun vs. Verb Form Confusion
**Question:** The board of directors was delighted (I) / to observe that the company managed to (II) / turnover a massive profit in the very first quarter. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). The single-word "turnover" is a noun and cannot be used as an action verb after the infinitive marker "to." The correct form must be the two-word phrasal verb **"turn over."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। एक शब्द वाला "turnover" एक संज्ञा (noun) है और इन्फिनिटिव "to" के बाद इसका उपयोग क्रिया के रूप में नहीं किया जा सकता। यहाँ क्रिया के रूप में दो अलग-अलग शब्दों वाले फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"turn over"** का प्रयोग होना चाहिए।

#### Q8. Spot the Error — Passive Voice Trap with "Turn out"
**Question:** The ambitious merger plan, (I) / which had been heavily criticized by financial experts, (II) / was turned out to be the most lucrative deal of the decade. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). As explained in Grammar Spotlight 1 of Post 41 and reiterated in this guide, "turn out" when meaning "to prove to be" is an intransitive copular verb and cannot be constructed in the passive voice. The auxiliary verb "was" must be removed to make it "turned out to be."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। जब "turn out" का अर्थ "साबित होना" होता है, तो यह एक अकर्मक क्रिया होती है और इसका पैसिव वॉइस में प्रयोग नहीं किया जा सकता। यहाँ "was turned out to be" के स्थान पर केवल सक्रिय रूप **"turned out to be"** होना चाहिए।

#### Q10. Spot the Error — Separable Pronoun Violation
**Question:** The police officer told the suspect (I) / to take the stolen documents from his pocket (II) / and turn over them immediately to the station clerk. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (III). "Turn over" (meaning to hand over or surrender) is a transitive separable phrasal verb. When the direct object is a pronoun (here, "them" referring to the documents), it must be placed between the verb "turn" and the particle "over." Therefore, "turn over them" must be corrected to **"turn them over."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। यहाँ चोरी के दस्तावेज पुलिस को "सौंपने या सरेंडर करने" का संदर्भ है। "Turn over" एक separable (विभाजित होने वाला) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। जब इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई सर्वनाम होता है (यहाँ "them"), तो उसे हमेशा वर्ब और पार्टिकल के बीच में रखा जाना चाहिए। अतः "turn over them" के स्थान पर **"turn them over"** होना चाहिए।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Turn out" और "Turn up" के अर्थों में मुख्य व्यावहारिक अंतर क्या है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों के बीच मुख्य व्यावहारिक अंतर इस प्रकार है:
1. **Turn up:** इसका प्रयोग मुख्य रूप से किसी व्यक्ति या खोई हुई वस्तु के अचानक मिलने, प्रकट होने या कहीं पहुँचने के लिए किया जाता है (जैसे: *My keys finally turned up*).
2. **Turn out:** इसका प्रयोग मुख्य रूप से किसी परिस्थिति या योजना के अंतिम परिणाम या "साबित होने" के लिए किया जाता है (जैसे: *The plan turned out to be successful*).

### Q2. व्यापार के संदर्भ में "Turn over" का क्या अर्थ होता है?
**Answer:** व्यावसायिक संदर्भ में "Turn over" का अर्थ होता है **"सामान बेचना या एक निश्चित मात्रा में राजस्व (revenue) उत्पन्न करना"**। उदाहरण के लिए, यदि कोई फर्म कहती है कि वे प्रति वर्ष 1 करोड़ रुपये का टर्नओवर करते हैं, तो इसका अर्थ है कि वे उतने मूल्य का माल बेचते हैं।

### Q3. "Turn in" का प्रयोग किन दो अलग-अलग संदर्भों में किया जा सकता है?
**Answer:** "Turn in" के दो बहुत ही सामान्य अर्थ हैं:
1. **सोने जाना (To go to bed):** अनौपचारिक रूप से रात को सोने जाने के लिए इसका प्रयोग होता है (जैसे: *I think I'll turn in now*).
2. **जमा करना या सौंपना (To submit/surrender):** किसी असाइनमेंट, रिपोर्ट को जमा करने या किसी अपराधी को पुलिस को सौंपने के लिए इसका प्रयोग होता है (जैसे: *He turned in his homework*).
