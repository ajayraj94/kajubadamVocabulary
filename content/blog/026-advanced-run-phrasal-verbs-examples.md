# Advanced 'Run' Phrasal Verbs Explained with English-Hindi Examples

In premium competitive examinations like **SSC CGL Tier 2**, **IBPS PO**, and **SBI PO Mains**, examiners frequently move beyond basic vocabulary to test highly sophisticated phrasal verbs. While basic "Run" variants like *run out* (exhausting supplies) are well-known, advanced forms like **"run up against"** (facing a difficult obstacle), **"run up"** (accumulating a massive debt quickly), and **"run out on"** (abandoning someone in need) are highly tested.

A minor contextual shift can completely alter the grammatical behavior and semantic meaning of these verbs. For instance, confusing "run on" (continuing without a break) with "run through" (spending money wastefully) is a common error in cloze tests. ## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: Prepositional Stacking in "Run up against" vs. "Run out on"
Examiners frequently exploit the structural complexity of three-word phrasal verbs (consisting of a verb, an adverb particle, and a preposition) by dropping the middle or final preposition.

*   **The Rule:** Three-word phrasal verbs like **"run up against"** (to face obstacles) and **"run out on"** (to abandon) function as single transitively cohesive units. They are **strictly inseparable** and require both prepositions to carry their specific meanings.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** The developmental committee ran against severe land acquisition hurdles. (Without "up," the phrasal verb is incomplete and grammatically incorrect).
*   ✅ **Correct:** The developmental committee **ran up against** severe land acquisition hurdles.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** He was criticized for running out his business partners during the financial crisis.
*   ✅ **Correct:** He was criticized for **running out on** his business partners during the financial crisis.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Polysemy Confusion in "Run on" — Power vs. Time
Advanced competitive examinations frequently test the dual applications of the phrasal verb **"run on."**

1.  **To be Powered by (Transitive Inseparable):** When followed by a resource or fuel, it describes the energy source.
    *   *Example:* Modern hybrid vehicles are designed to **run on** both electricity and gasoline.
2.  **To Continue without stopping (Intransitive):** When describing time, meetings, or speeches, it means continuing for an unexpectedly long period.
    *   *Example:* The annual general meeting **ran on** for four hours, delaying other scheduled sessions.
*   **The Trap:** In close tests, look at the subject. If the subject is a machine or vehicle, "run on" means fuel consumption. If the subject is an event, time, or speech, it means temporal extension.

**Question:** During the implementation of the clean energy project, the research team ________ unexpected technical difficulties with the grid integration.

(B) ran through
(C) ran up against

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**

#### Q2. Sentence Improvement — Accumulating Debt
**Question:** The extravagant manager managed to **run through** a massive bill of five thousand dollars at the luxury hotel.

**Options:**
(A) run up
(B) run up against
(C) run out on
(D) run off with
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The manager accumulated a massive bill at a luxury hotel. The correct phrasal verb meaning to accumulate a large debt or bill quickly is "run up." "Run through" means to spend money wastefully, which is grammatically awkward when directly paired with "a massive bill." Therefore, option (A) is the correct improvement.
- **Hindi:** यहाँ मैनेजर द्वारा एक लक्जरी होटल में पांच हजार डॉलर का एक बहुत बड़ा बिल "इकट्ठा करने या चढ़ाने" का संदर्भ है। बिल या कर्ज की बड़ी राशि तेजी से बढ़ाने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"run up"** होता है। "Run through" का अर्थ पैसे उड़ाना होता है, जो यहाँ "bill" शब्द के साथ सीधा व्याकरणिक रूप से फिट नहीं बैठता। इसलिए विकल्प (A) सही सुधार है।

#### Q3. Spot the Error — Pronoun Placement with "Run off"
**Question:** The supervisor approved the final draft (I) / of the circular and asked the secretary (II) / to run off them before the meeting starts. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - C**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (III). "Run off" (meaning to print or copy quickly) is a transitive separable phrasal verb. When the object of a separable phrasal verb is a pronoun (here, "them" referring to the drafts/copies), the pronoun must be placed in the middle. Therefore, "run off them" must be corrected to **"run them off."**
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (III) में है। "Run off" (प्रतियाँ तैयार/प्रिंट करना) एक separable (विभाजित होने वाला) फ्रेज़ल वर्ब है। जब इसका ऑब्जेक्ट कोई सर्वनाम होता है (यहाँ ड्राफ्ट्स के लिए "them"), तो उस सर्वनाम को हमेशा वर्ब और पार्टिकल के बीच में रखा जाना चाहिए। इसलिए, "run off them" के स्थान पर **"run them off"** होना चाहिए।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Run up against" और "Run out on" के प्रयोग में क्या बुनियादी अंतर है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों तीन शब्दों वाले फ्रेज़ल वर्ब्स में काफी बड़ा अंतर है:
1. **Run up against:** इसका अर्थ होता है किसी बड़ी समस्या, बाधा या सख्त नियम का सामना करना (जैसे: *We ran up against severe hurdles*).
2. **Run out on:** इसका अर्थ होता है जरूरत के समय अपने परिवार, मुवक्किल या दोस्तों को अधर में छोड़ देना या उनका साथ छोड़ देना (जैसे: *He ran out on his family*).

### Q2. "Run up" का उपयोग बिल या कर्ज के संदर्भ में कैसे किया जाता है?
**Answer:** "Run up" का अर्थ होता है बहुत तेज़ी से किसी बड़ी राशि को इकट्ठा करना या बढ़ाना। इसका प्रयोग मुख्य रूप से बिजली के बिल, क्रेडिट कार्ड के कर्ज या होटल के खर्चों के लिए किया जाता है जब वे बहुत अधिक बढ़ जाते हैं (जैसे: *He ran up a high hotel bill*).

### Q3. "Run on" का उपयोग मशीनों और बैठकों के संदर्भ में कैसे भिन्न होता है?
**Answer:** यह वाक्य के कर्ता (Subject) पर निर्भर करता है:
1. **ईंधन पर चलना (Powered by):** जब कर्ता कोई मशीन या वाहन हो (जैसे: *The car runs on solar power*).
2. **बहुत लंबा खिंचना (Continue non-stop):** जब कर्ता कोई बैठक, भाषण या चर्चा हो (जैसे: *The meeting ran on for four hours*).
