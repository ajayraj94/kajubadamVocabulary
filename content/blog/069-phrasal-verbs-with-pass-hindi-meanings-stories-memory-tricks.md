# Phrasal Verbs with 'Pass' with Hindi Meanings, Stories & Memory Tricks (Pass away, Pass out, Pass off) for SSC CGL, NDA, CDS & Bank PO

In major competitive examinations such as SSC CGL, SBI PO, IBPS PO, UPSC CDS, and NDA, phrasal verbs are a frequent source of errors for candidates. Among these, phrasal verbs based on the verb **"Pass"**—such as **"Pass away,"** **"Pass out,"** and **"Pass off"**—are highly tested. Because these expressions appear visually similar, candidates often confuse their meanings, leading to errors in sentence completion, error spotting, and reading comprehension sections.

Understanding the difference between a military cadet "passing out" and a situation "passing off" successfully is crucial for securing high marks. To help you build a clear, long-term understanding, the academic editorial team at **kajubadamvocabulary.in** has compiled this bilingual guide. By combining our contextual story method, structured tables, and deep grammatical analysis, you can learn to use these phrasal verbs accurately.

## 🔍 Advanced Grammar Spotlights — व्याकरण के मुख्य नियम

### ⚡ Spotlight 1: The Polysemous Nature of "Pass out" and "Pass off"
In vocabulary sections, examiners often exploit the multiple, distinct meanings of **"Pass out"** and **"Pass off."**

*   **Pass out (Dual Meanings):**
    1.  **Fainting:** To lose consciousness.
        *   *Example:* He **passed out** due to severe dehydration during the marathon.
    2.  **Graduating:** To successfully complete a course at a military or service academy.
        *   *Example:* The newly trained officers **passed out** of the academy yesterday.
*   **Pass off (Dual Meanings):**
    1.  **Event Execution:** To take place or happen in a specific way.
        *   *Example:* The annual festival **passed off** beautifully despite the rain.
    2.  **Impersonation/Pretense:** To misrepresent someone or something as something else. This construction is transitive and separable, requiring the structure: *pass [someone/something] off as [something else]*.
        *   *Example:* He tried to **pass himself off** as a certified surgeon.

### ⚡ Spotlight 2: Separability and Transitivity with "Pass over" vs. "Pass on"
Understanding the transitivity of **"Pass over"** and **"Pass on"** is essential for answering sentence structure questions correctly.

*   **The Rule:** 
    1.  **Pass over** when meaning "to ignore someone for a promotion" is strictly **transitive and separable**. If the object is a pronoun, it must be placed between "pass" and "over."
        *   ❌ **Incorrect:** The board of directors decided to pass over him for the manager's role.
        *   ✅ **Correct:** The board of directors decided to **pass him over** for the manager's role.
    2.  **Pass on** can be **intransitive** when used as a polite euphemism for dying (synonymous with *pass away*). However, when it means "to transmit information or transfer objects," it is **transitive and separable**.
        *   *Example (Intransitive):* The patient peacefully **passed on** in the early hours of the morning.
        *   *Example (Transitive):* Please **pass the message on** to the team leader.

#### Q1. Spot the Error — Semantic Word Swap Trap (SSC CGL Tier-2 Level)
**Question:** During the grueling cross-country race, (I) / several elite athletes passed down (II) / due to severe dehydration and high heat. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error lies in part (II). The phrasal verb meaning to lose consciousness or faint is "pass out," not "pass down." "Pass down" means to hand down a legacy or tradition through generations. Therefore, "passed down" must be replaced with "passed out" to restore the correct meaning of the sentence.
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (II) में है। बेहोश होने या संज्ञा खोने के अर्थ में सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब "pass out" (भूतकाल "passed out") होता है, न कि "pass down"। "Pass down" का अर्थ परंपरा या विरासत को अगली पीढ़ी को सौंपना होता है। अतः वाक्य को अर्थपूर्ण और व्याकरणिक रूप से शुद्ध बनाने के लिए "passed down" के स्थान पर "passed out" का प्रयोग किया जाना चाहिए।

(A) passed off / passed over
(B) passed away / passed out

This makes option (B) the only contextually and grammatically correct option.

#### Q3. Sentence Improvement — Pretense Context (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** The con artist tried to **pass over** himself as a certified financial advisor to swindle the unsuspecting investors.

**Options:**
(A) pass off
(B) pass out
(C) pass up
(D) pass down
(E) No correction required

**Correct Answer - A**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The sentence describes a con artist pretending to be or misrepresenting himself as a certified financial advisor. The correct phrasal verb meaning to pretend to be someone else or to misrepresent is "pass off" (specifically, "pass oneself off as"). "Pass over" means to ignore for a promotion or skip, which does not fit here. Therefore, option (A) is the correct improvement.
- **Hindi:** वाक्य में एक ठग (con artist) द्वारा धोखा देने के लिए स्वयं को प्रमाणित वित्तीय सलाहकार के रूप में "दिखाने या ढोंग करने" का संदर्भ है। किसी अन्य व्यक्ति के रूप में ढोंग करने या गलत तरीके से खुद को पेश करने के लिए सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"pass off"** (pass oneself off as) होता है। "Pass over" का अर्थ पदोन्नति के लिए नजरअंदाज करना होता है, जो यहाँ असंगत है। अतः विकल्प (A) सही सुधार है।

(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)

#### Q8. Spot the Error — Intransitive Passive Voice Trap (UPSC CDS Level)
**Question:** The annual corporate seminar (I) / was passed off successfully (II) / despite the initial technical difficulties in the main auditorium. (III)

**Options:**
(A) Only (I)
(B) Only (II)
(C) Only (III)
(D) Both (II) and (III)
(E) No Error

**Correct Answer - B**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** The error is in part (II). The phrasal verb "pass off" meaning "to happen or take place" is strictly intransitive. Intransitive phrasal verbs cannot be used in passive voice constructions. Therefore, using the auxiliary verb "was" before "passed off" is grammatically incorrect; it must be corrected to the active past tense "passed off successfully."
- **Hindi:** त्रुटि वाक्य के भाग (II) में है। किसी घटना या कार्यक्रम के "आयोजित होने या संपन्न होने" के अर्थ में "pass off" एक अकर्मक क्रिया (intransitive verb) है। अकर्मक क्रियाओं का पैसिव वॉइस नहीं बनाया जा सकता, इसलिए यहाँ "passed off" के पहले "was" का प्रयोग अशुद्ध है। इसे सुधारकर पास्ट इंडेफिनिट एक्टिव वॉइस में केवल **"passed off successfully"** लिखा जाना चाहिए।

**Correct Answer - D**
**Detailed Explanation:**
- **English:** All three sentences are grammatically and syntactically correct. Sentence (I) correctly uses "passed off" to mean took place or happened. Sentence (II) correctly uses "passed away" to mean died. Sentence (III) correctly uses "passed over" to mean bypassed or ignored for a position.
- **Hindi:** दिए गए तीनों वाक्य व्याकरण और संदर्भ की दृष्टि से पूरी तरह से शुद्ध हैं। वाक्य (I) में समारोह के "संपन्न होने" के अर्थ में "passed off" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (II) में बिल्ली की मृत्यु के अर्थ में "passed away" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। वाक्य (III) में वरिष्ठ उम्मीदवार को पद के लिए "नजरअंदाज करने" के अर्थ में "passed over" का सही प्रयोग हुआ है। अतः विकल्प (D) सही उत्तर है।

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1. "Pass over" और "Pass up" के बीच मुख्य अंतर क्या है?
**Answer:** इन दोनों के बीच अंतर को उनके संदर्भ से समझा जा सकता है:
*   **Pass over:** इसका प्रयोग तब होता है जब किसी व्यक्ति को किसी नौकरी या पदोन्नति (promotion) के लिए **नजरअंदाज या बाईपास** कर दिया जाता है (जैसे: *He was passed over for the job*).
*   **Pass up:** इसका प्रयोग तब होता है जब आप स्वयं किसी अच्छे अवसर या प्रस्ताव को **अस्वीकार कर देते हैं या छोड़ देते हैं** (जैसे: *Never pass up a good opportunity*).

### Q2. क्या "Pass out" का प्रयोग पैसिव वॉइस (Passive Voice) में किया जा सकता है?
**Answer:** नहीं, "Pass out" (बेहोश होना या दीक्षित होना) एक अकर्मक क्रिया (intransitive phrasal verb) है। चूंकि अकर्मक क्रियाओं का कोई प्रत्यक्ष कर्म (direct object) नहीं होता, इसलिए व्याकरण के नियमों के अनुसार इनका पैसिव वॉइस बनाना पूरी तरह अशुद्ध माना जाता है।

### Q3. "He tried to pass over himself as a doctor" लिखना गलत क्यों है?
**Answer:** यहाँ "ढोंग करने या खुद को गलत तरीके से पेश करने" का संदर्भ है। इस विशिष्ट अर्थ के लिए सही फ्रेज़ल वर्ब **"pass off"** (pass oneself off as) होता है, न कि "pass over"। अतः शुद्ध वाक्य **"He tried to pass himself off as a doctor"** होगा।
